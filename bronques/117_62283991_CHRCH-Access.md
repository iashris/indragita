##    🔑 Artist Bait: Ongoing Narrative 

![](https://uploads.teachablecdn.com/attachments/04050ba1ecff41b399a8fbb9fdb6119f.avif)

_Chelsea Girls, 1966_

Once you [figure out what you want your relationship with your audience to look like](https://learn.makeartnotcontent.com/courses/enroll/lectures/62198905), you finally have the secret for sharing with intention.

But still, that doesn’t mean that you will instantly know WHAT to share with your people. It may take a few weeks to settle into your new skin.

Look at what you share with your audience right now. Notice that there's no connective tissue tying everything together? It's all "singular posts" vying for people's attention.

And because of that, when you share something, it's almost like you're starting from scratch every time.

What's worse is that-- if someone, by chance, discovers your work online, there's nothing built-in to propel them to go discover everything else.

Today we're going to change that.  


  


#  **RAGE BAIT VS ARTIST BAIT**  


The pre-AI era was easy to tame. Your online presence was all about “your portfolio” and every week you’d add to it.   


But today, sharing random posts does very little for an artist. Even if one of them goes viral. So what? Virality without a body of work waiting for new potential fans gets you nowhere.  


Today, your content graveyard… Oops, sorry sorry, I meant… Your “portfolio”… As it stands, cannot compete against the current online slop it’s pitted against on your feed.  


Artists need “online slop” insurance. Fortunately for us, it isn’t that hard to build.  


The OPPORTUNITY right now, ironically, is to actually do exactly what you’ve secretly wanted to do since the social media was invented:  


**Be more human, more authentic, more raw, and more real to get people’s attention.  **  


Nowadays, everyone thinks this means that they have to be outrageous. They think they need to “rage-bait” for attention. They think they have to create content designed to get "a reaction". But that’s empty calories. Zero depth.  


That's not YOU.  


Luckily for artists, you can WIN just as big by doing the exact opposite.   


_By creating ARTIST-BAIT_

  


#  **WHAT IS ARTIST-BAIT?**  


ARTIST-BAIT is when you…  


1\. Create media that slows people down and makes them feel seen.

2\. Share things that nurture community, and specific values.

3\. Decelerate the viewer. Focus on more nuanced emotions instead of trying to be confrontational like others do. 

4\. Share art that is pushes your art form forward. Creating work that is so undeniable people have to check it out.

5\. Create media that makes people feel wiser after they come across you. Or that makes them want to go “touch grass” and reconnect to their essence.

Because the content creators have swung the pendulum is soooo far to the other side... This is your time to shine by doing the exact opposite.  


My friend Gabi, [@sighswoon](https://www.instagram.com/sighswoon/) does exactly that. She “traps” you by using the aesthetics of the internet, and before you know it, you’ve gone down her rabbit hole of intellectual and spiritual musings.   


Yes, it’s absolutely possible to use reverse-slop psychology for "good".  


But to do any of that, you’ll have to stop being “middle of the road” like you have been these last few years.  


The artist’s insurance against RAGE BAIT is sitting down to figure out, once and for all, what will be your ONGOING NARRATIVE online.   


_ONGOING NARRATIVE is the perfect ARTIST BAIT._

  


#  **WHAT IS ONGOING NARRATIVE?**  


Now that you know what your artist/audience relationship is, the next thing to figure out is… What is the ONGOING NARRATIVE that matches it?  


AN ONGOING NARRATIVE IS A REPEATABLE FORMULA (aka FORMAT) FOR PRESENTING YOUR WORK THAT MAKES PEOPLE TUNE IN TO SEE WHAT YOU WILL DO NEXT. You should be able to describe this formula in one simple sentence. “Tune in to see…”

Right now, all your online presence is a collection of disconnected things you've shared online. But switching your method to ONGOING NARRATIVE unifies all your work into one cohesive whole. When you do this, your online presence immediately gives off an aura of success.   


An ONGOING NARRATIVE creates a habit with your audience.  


Having an ONGOING NARRATIVE is the cheat code for never being at a loss about what to post.   


Your ONGOING NARRATIVE is the frame you will put your whole body of work online in. It’s how you will “package” everything that you do so it beats the “content slop” you’re surrounded by online.   


You love examples. So let use the aforementioned [**@sighswoon**](https://www.instagram.com/sighswoon/) (There’s a reason why she is the only person I’ve ever co-hosted a 'Make art not content' podcast with!

**SHE IS PURE TMS✨):**  


1\. @sighswoon’s art is writing.

2\. @sighswoon has a “transformational” relationship with her audience. 

3\. @sighswoon’s ONGOING NARRATIVE takes place on her Patreon.

4\. @sighswoon's FORMULA = "Dropping knowledge". It is a weekly appointment to help other female artists make sense of being spiritual in a material world. She’s a young “guru” who has none of the negative patriarchal tendencies the word “guru” implies. She has the confidence to teach even while she is still discovering things herself.

4\. This format works for @sighswoon because her audience can't wait to hear what new gems of knowledge she will drop to open their minds. She uses Instagram and Discord for microdosing the audience so that they go down the rabbit hole that leads to her amazing Patreon.  


Because she has established an ONGOING NARRATIVE, figuring out what to share is fun and easy.  


That’s why you need one of those “ongoing narrative” thing-a-ma-jiggies too!  


I know, I know… You love examples. So here’s another. These kids totally get it:  


[CREEK CRAWLERS _](https://www.instagram.com/creek_crawlers39?igsh=YjluaW5sb3Q3cTQ3)  


Talk about living up to “Emma Summer”!  


In their first video (pinned) they gave their audience something (aspirational) to latch on to. And it turned out to be so compelling to their fans that they beat their goal of “10k by the end of the summer”— IN A FEW WEEKS! 

  


#  **HOW DO YOU COME UP WITH YOUR ONGOING NARRATIVE?**  


The ONGOING NARRATIVE you will design this week will be the first of many “eras”.  


But how do you come up with this first one?  


Well, first, you need to understand who the star of the show is.  


_In_** _aspirational_** _artist/audience relationships, the main character is YOU, YOUR “SCENE”, AND/OR YOUR CITY.  _  


_In_** _inspirational_** _artist/audience relationships, the main character is YOUR ART and the emotions it brings._  


_In_** _transformational_** _artist/audience relationships, the main character is YOUR AUDIENCE and the epiphanies they will receive through your worldview.  _  


**Whatever format you choose should focus on the corresponding main character.  **

  


#  **“FOLLOW ME TO WATCH ME CREATE MY ART” IS NOT GOOD ENOUGH, SORRY**  


_“Follow along as I create art…”, or any variation of that sentence, is not a good enough “ongoing narrative”. EVERYONE thinks they’re an artist these days. So that won't be enough to stand out. No one cares about another random person making art._

_You’re gonna have to come up with something with more layers than just “you making art”._

_Today, an artist’s ONGOING NARRATIVE needs to have a REPEATABLE and compelling HOOK. A HOOK is that catchy thing about how you present yourself/your art online that gets people excited. Something that makes them want to binge-watch everything you make once they discover you_. And pretty soon… Wanna see everything you’ll make in the future.   


If you can do a good job drawing them into your ONGOING NARRATIVE, they will get addicted to your art.  


They will look forward to being part of whatever artist/audience relationship you’ve established with them.

_You will have a new fan… For all the right reasons._

  


#  **REPEAT AFTER ME**  


Aspirational artist/audience relationships: **“I need to find an online format that makes my art persona bingeable.”**  


Inspirational artist/audience relationships: **“I need to find an online format that makes my art process bingeable.”**  


Transformational artist/audience relationships: **“I need to find an online format that makes my art worldview bingeable.”**  


Get it?  


But please, people… STOP WITH THE LISTS!!  


Every time artists describe something they wanna do, they always use a long list of adjectives. Stop that! Long lists just show me (and the audience) that you’re still not committed. That you’re afraid of choosing. You’re trying to please everyone. You’re trying to be a Chinese menu again.  


NO LISTS Y’ALL!  


Give your audience ONE thing to latch onto that encapsulates the purpose of your channel.  


_Figure out an ONGOING NARRATIVE that reflects the nature of the relationship you want to establish with them in this new era of yours._

  


#  **ONGOING NARRATIVE FORMULAS**  


**“I love what that artist is doing so much! Once I watch one of their videos, I wanna watch them all.”**  


===  


FORMULA: RAGE BAIT & SWITCH (aspirational artist/audience relationship)  


DESCRIPTION: “While the world was arguing about “(typical rage bait topic)”, here’s what I was doing instead…” Followed by: an artistic video essay of quiet moments returning to the essence of things.  


WHY IT WORKS: Uses rage-bait framing but delivers uplifting ARTIST BAIT instead. Starts with the same opening line to give the audience a sense of familiarity every time.  


===  


FORMULA: AN IMPOSSIBLE GOAL (inspirational artist/audience relationship)  


DESCRIPTION: Another friend of mine, Cocovan ([@iamcocovan](https://www.instagram.com/iamcocovan/)) started [@theworldletter](https://www.instagram.com/theworldletter/) a few years ago. Her goal is to get people from every nation to write a letter to the world and expose her final performance artwork scroll in a gallery at the end.  


WHY IT WORKS: You want to see her progression on this massive undertaking. The goal is clear. It involves strangers. Involving strangers is a cheat code to keeping an ONGOING NARRATIVE exciting.

===  


FORMULA: CATCHPHRASE FRAMED ADVICE (transformational artist/audience relationship)  


DESCRIPTION: Find a catchphrase that you can make your very own like: “I don’t know who needs to hear this, but…” followed by your art-inspired wisdom.  


WHY IT WORKS: Familiar opening line → unexpected drop of emotional truth It feels like a welcome interruption from everything else. YOU become the artist on their feed that can break the hypnotic spell doomscrolling. 

===  


FORMULA: “IT LOOKED DIFFERENT IN MY HEAD” (inspirational artist/audience relationship)  


DESCRIPTION: Show them what you were attempting to do, then show them what it actually turned out like.  


WHY IT WORKS: Curiosity hook. People want to wait around to see the result. 

===

HINT: Your ONGOING FORMULA doesn't even have to be based on the art. It could also be based around the world you're building that contains the art! Figure out a cute recurring formula that amplifies this “world” you’re building around the art. That way, when the corresponding art drops, it will feel completely natural to your audience and like it's the last piece of the puzzle.  


CHEAT CODE: Any formula that includes APPROACHING STRANGERS is immediately bingeable if done well. People love “unscripted”. AI can’t generate this kind of human authenticity. It’s the only online narrative that is IMMUNE TO AI.  


  


# **COLOR YOUR ONLINE NARRATIVE  **

NONE OF THIS SECTION MATTERS IF YOU DON'T HAVE A RECURRING FORMAT.

NONE OF THIS IS A SHORTCUT TO FIGURING OUT A RECURRING FORMAT.

Got it? Let's continue...

In this new era, yes, you can show up less (like once a week minimum), but now you have to make your appearances more impactful.  


_Pick the home base of where you’re going to be sharing your ONGOING NARRATIVE, and then make all of your other appearances online lead to where this main show is taking place._  


EMAIL, YOUTUBE, INSTAGRAM, a PODCAST, or whatever.  


Give your series a name. Pin the first episode. Make a highlight.  


Stick to a voice. Keep the same tone and the same emotional rhythm from piece to piece.  


**Give this new era a name.**  


Give your audience a name.  


Use Visual anchors so that when people see you while scrolling online, they immediately know it’s you.  


Use the same fonts everywhere.  


  


#  **YOUR TURN. AND I MEAN, LIKE, THIS WEEK!**  


There are millions of ONGOING NARRATIVES that you can choose to frame your art online.  


You’re creative. You’ll figure it out.  


And the cool thing is, once you do, you never have to worry about “What am I going to post?” anymore. And your whole online presence will now have structure. You’ll know how to show up for your people. Improving with each appearance.   


But the temptation right now will be to take a long break to “figure all this out”.  


**No, no, no! Don’t fall for the “resistance”!**  


Trust me on this one… Figuring out your ONGOING NARRATIVE will never happen by “thinking about it”. It will only happen by taking action. By testing, making versions, iterating and STAYING ON STAGE IN FRONT OF THE CLASS (your audience).

There's no reason that you can't come up with an ongoing narrative format TODAY.  


And… Once you get 75% of that idea… Go shoot version 1 immediately. All you need is a good-enough premise. You won’t know if it's a GREAT premise until you execute that first one. Figuring out the other 25% will only happen after the sweat, effort… When you give yourself and your team/confidantes/advisors something concrete to analyze.  


Promise me.  


Go do version one THIS WEEK!  


_Don't overthink yourself out of taking immediate action again._
