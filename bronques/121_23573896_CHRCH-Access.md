##    18\. WORLD BUILDING (PART IV): YOUR AUDIENCE... NO, IT ISN'T 'EVERYBODY' 

"Dear Bronques, Everyone I meet tells me they love my art. But for some reason my work doesn't seem to be connecting with the masses? What am I missing?"

Most creatives spend all day thinking about art.

Their art.

The art that influences them.

The competition's art.

That's why they end up creating what's known as "art for art's sake". 

But one day they wake up and realize "Nothings really happening". And they're confused. They know their art is "amazing"... I mean, all sorts of people tell them that their art is "amazing"... But for some reason they can't turn it into a career. 

_That's because "getting paid for your art" requires a different skillset than making "art for art's sake"._

To get paid for what you do, you have to respect the holy trinity.

  


# 1\. Your Art 

# 2\. Your Audience

# 3\. Your Message

  


I'm guessing you have your art on point.

And I hope to God you've found your message (the reason you do what you do).

But have you ever asked yourself the million-dollar question:

**Who are you making this art for?**

I'm talking beyond your friends, your family, your boyfriend and all the lonely people that have been following you on Instagram since high school.

What I'm asking you is: What type of complete strangers... Would fall in love with what you do?

If you're thinking "everybody"... That's not a good sign.

_You need to know who your audience REALLY is if you wanna make this your career._

_Do you think when The Weeknd launched 'House Of Balloons' that he was making music for "everybody"?_

_Do you think when Dave Chappelle writes stand-up, that he does it to please "everybody"?_

_Do you think "SoundCloud rappers" get face tattoos that make them unrecognizable to their mothers to be in "everybody's" good books?_

_Then why are YOU are trying to appeal to the largest group you can? When the fastest path to your success is the complete opposite?_

It's crazy but it's true: You need to make art that appeals to the SMALLEST (viable) group possible.

If you want to make this a career.

A group small enough to be feverishly united but big enough to matter and have a financial impact. 

And you do that by creating art that's so unique to you that only that. small. group. could love it (for now).

Art that your biggest fans will tell outsiders that "they wouldn't understand."

Ok... Question.

I've asked this before elsewhere, but I need to ask it again here. How many of your followers would notice if you completely stopped posting on social media? Well... THAT'S how many REAL followers you have.

If you don’t have a pocket of people that would mourn the loss of your art, then you haven't brought value to the world yet. You don't have a business.

That "exchange value" is how you make money in this "creativity business".

You're not looking for casual listeners. Causal viewers. Casual readers. 

You need to develop a niche of fans (or clients) who believe that you're the "bees knees" WAY before the masses catch on.

That "pocket of people" is what they call in marketing lingo: "your niche".

Figure out your niche. And go after them putting blinders on to the rest of humanity.

It's kinda like fishing. 

You have to be the fisherman with the specialized bait, that's trying to catch cod.

Yes, I know you’ll see many juicy salmon swim by. But it’s ok, because you’ve decided to catch cod. Stay focused.

And now because you know what you're looking for... And because you have the right bait (thin slices of squid on your hook), you're gonna catch more cod than anyone else that's using "general bait" just hoping to catch ANY fish.

And people will think you're "lucky" for catching all that cod.

And soon they will line up to buy your cod. (Even the salmon fishermen.)

And eventually, you’ll need a street team to help you run your cod business.

And people will call you "the cod guy".

And you'll make it on 'Ellen'.

  


## Is it sinking in?

  


# 1\. Figure out who your "pocket of people" are.

# 2\. Generously share your unique gifts with them and only them in mind.

# 3\. Unapologetically ignore the opinion of the masses who "don't understand" anyways. And for now they shouldn't if you're doing it right.

# 4\. Watch as the masses eventually come to you anyways.

  


It will take patience... And discipline.

But...

That is the actual "shortcut". 

That is the "origin story" of all the great artists we love.

But once you know your AUDIENCE and you know the ATTRACTION TOOLS to get them into your WORLD...

How do you get them there? How do you turn this audience into fans?

[You do it by using a completely revolutionary new way of looking at content creation.](https://learn.lastnightsparty.tv/courses/enroll/lectures/23551753)
