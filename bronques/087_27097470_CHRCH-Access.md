##    5\. Your Fans Are Everything. So, Start Acting Like it (Part I): Your Art Doesn't "Matter" (Yet) 

I don't care if you're "the next Supreme"...

I don't care if you're about to take "the hairdressing community by storm "...

Or if you're a caffein induced stylist that can "pull from Telfar"... 

Or if you're a COVID-inspired fashion photographer...

Or if you're the "Trap Bob Dylan"...

Or if you're the only architect who's mastered the timeless principles of Christopher Alexander...

You are nothing.

Without your fans.

You will not advance.

Without your fans.

Put this up on your wall where you can see it every day:

## YOUR CAREER IS NOT YOUR ART. YOUR CAREER IS YOUR RELATIONSHIP TO YOUR FANS.

I know, I know.

You think that what you do is "special".

But it's only gonna be "special" when there's a passionate group of people who says it is.

And I don't mean your family or your friends. 

I mean the people who become your NEW "family and friends" BECAUSE OF your art.

I don't just mean the people who buy what you do.

I also mean the people who will be on your team one day to help you promote it.

For example:

I meet so many photographers trying to get known.

They put their pics on Instagram.

It's their "portfolio", they say.

Dear photographers, Did you know that 95 million photos get uploaded every day on Instagram?

Why should we care about yours?

Ok, I'll come back to that in a second.

Another statistic to make you weep: 

4000 songs get added to Spotify every day. 

I meet singers and producers trying to get known. Some even have labels behind them.

Um, tell me: What about YOUR new single makes it something that I have to hear before I die?

You see, THAT'S the problem.

You can't answer that question in one sentence.

You can't answer that question with conviction.

You can't give me an answer that makes me start typing your name into Spotify while you're still talking.

You have no story.

You have no message.

If you had either of those things, you'd be creating better odds for yourself. You'd have your own lane. 

Let me show you how--

No seriously. Let's do the math.

We'll use music as an example.

**But this applies to anyone out there with a brand.**

So... 4,000 songs released a day, right? Of those 4,000 releases, how many of them are "pop artists"?

Maybe 2000.

How many of them are "pop artists who rap"?

Maybe 500.

How many are "pop artists who rap exclusively about female empowerment"?

Maybe 150.

How many are "pop artists who rap exclusively about female empowerment, who have blue hair, and have been releasing consistently for the last 3 years, and never disappeared to go on 'normal people vacations' but have gone "all in" to keep relentless communication their fans documenting all the highs and the lows along the way"?

One.

[Ashnikko.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashnikko)

What I'm trying to tell you is that you need a story.

What I'm trying to tell you is that you need to nurture your audience.

Your "people".

You have to be like that Presets song... SING IT:

## "I'm here with all of my people

## Locked up with all of my people

## So let me hear you scream if you're with me"

You have to know in fine detail who you're doing this for.

Otherwise, your lyrics will suck.

Your pictures will suck.

Your art will suck.

Your brand will suck.

_Creative work sucks when it isn't made for anyone in particular._

Yuck.

It sucks when... You're trying to appeal to "everybody".

Gross.

It makes you vanilla.

Instead, what you want is for your fans to say, "I feel like you made this just for me."

That's what I was talking about in this module: 

[LE$$XN 02: Every Brand Should Have A Muse ](https://learn.lastnightsparty.tv/courses/enroll/lectures/9022531)

But you only read it. You understood it, but you didn't DO anything about it.

It gave you "motivation",... And then you forgot about it when your friend texted you to go to the new Ramen spot.

[But reading is not action. That may have been all you needed for the other modules but you can't get away with that in 'The Book Of Money'.](https://learn.lastnightsparty.tv/courses/enroll/lectures/22983261)

You need to grab a pen and paper so you can figure this shit out once and for all.

That's what a PR person would do if you had the money to hire one.

[YOUR FANS ARE EVERYTHING. START ACTING LIKE IT (PART II)](https://learn.lastnightsparty.tv/courses/enroll/lectures/27097903)

Don't click that on that link until you have a pen and paper.

And when you do... Don't fucking just "read through it"!

This is not about "motivation" anymore.

It's about action.

The type of action that makes you feel like you've moved the needle in your career today.

This is the stuff no-one calls you out on. Good luck finding anything like what's in this 'action sheet' anywhere else on the internet.

Why? 'Cause I made this for MY people.

Got your pen and paper. Let's go:

[YOUR FANS ARE EVERYTHING. START ACTING LIKE IT (PART II)](https://learn.lastnightsparty.tv/courses/enroll/lectures/27097903)
