##    🔑 21. The Copywriting Module 

A student started one of my favorite YouTube channels a few years ago. It was about going back to her roots and reconnecting to her cultural heritage. Video by video. So good! Her channel would inspire others to do the same and discover a new world through her eyes.  


But every video got further and further away from the original “mission statement” she had started with.  


So that’s when I asked her the million-dollar question…  


 _Which I will get to in a second._  


Another student sent me his crowdfunding page for the new movie he’s trying to make. In the email, the project sounded promising, until I got to the crowdfunding website… Then my energy drained.   


So I asked HIM the million-dollar question also…  


 _Which I will get to in a second._  


Most artists believe that the world works in the following way: You share your art, and either the world likes it or it doesn’t. If people like it, you become successful. If they don’t, it’s time to find a “real job”. This deranged artist myth is why so many creative people are on an emotional roller coaster. One day, you’re confident. The next day, you don’t know if you’ll EVER be good enough.   


The artist myth is that people are “caught” by art that moves them. All in one sweeping moment of grand emotion.   


I wish.  


Sure, that happens sometimes. But only when someone’s defenses are completely down. Like they’re kissing the love of their life, and your song happens to come on. Or they’re backpacking in Europe, they walk into a gallery cafe, they see your little painting in the window sill, and they buy it.    


But those instances are rare. Nowadays, people’s defenses are way up.  


Personally, I like how crazy it is.  


Because it’s truthful. You know that if you somehow manage to get an audience’s attention, you know it’s real. No one (except maybe your friends and family) has the energy to pat you on the back, or look at what you’re doing unless you “catch them, then move them”.   


The audience doesn’t lie. When they are moved they will tell you. Or even better… They’ll be speechless. You will feel it in the air. If you don’t get that kind of positive reaction, then they weren’t moved.  


But many artists don’t realize that moving the audience isn’t phase I. It’s Phase II.  


You have to “catch them” first. You have to get their attention.  


“Artists are no better than porn stars or influencers. You’re all in the same business, which is… ‘Seeking Attention’.” — Bob Lefsetz   


That’s what makes art agents and record labels so desirable. Their only job is to make sure your art is seen by people. They can’t make people like it, but they can make sure it gets its time in the sun. Once they’ve done that part, it’s up to the charisma of your art to do the rest.   


The problem is, you have to CATCH those gatekeepers, too, before they will work on your behalf.   


But maybe you’re thinking, I don’t want to catch anyone, I just want to be creative. You’d be missing the point. ALL OF IT is creative, babe. Imagine saying “I only want to make music. I don’t care about the album art” (the thing that catches some people BEFORE they even hear the music). Every step is creative if you let it be. Your instagram should be creative. Your emails should be creative. Your promo videos should be creative. Your website… If you let them be.  


When your attempts don’t work at first, don’t cry. This is only feedback. Just try again, but in another way. That’s the journey. It’s called iteration.   


If you’re not getting any likes on Instagram it’s not because you suck. It’s because you haven’t figured out how to make people care enough to stop. You see? That’s a different problem.  


If no one is adding your songs to playlists, or using them for sync licenses, it’s because they have no clue you even exist. You see? That’s a different problem.  


You’ve been putting so much focus on trying to move people with your art that you never bothered to improve the skills of how to make them NOTICE YOU.   


The cart before the horse.  


Before you can “move them”, you have to “catch them”.  


But what I want to get through to you is this… don’t judge your art yet. Not at this phase of the game. No one has even seen it. You’re still learning how to get through the door, remember? THEY HAVEN’T EVEN SEEN IT. You didn’t “catch them” yet.  


Have you ever seen a child get excited about what they’re about to show you BEFORE they unveil it? That’s them trying to “catch you” first. They want your full attention before they show you anything. Children understand how it works.  


But how do you do that? How do you catch people’s attention? How do you get an audience… Or gatekeepers… Or even collaborators… To notice you? Especially when you’re brand new?  


Have you ever seen a child get excited about what they’re about to show you BEFORE they unveil it? That’s them trying to “catch you” first. They want your full attention before they show you anything. Children understand how it works.  


There are only three ways.

  


#  **1\. PHOTOGRAPHY**  


You don’t need to become a “photographer”. You only need to become great at using photography in a way that serves your art. Invest in finding ways of photographing your art that a professional photographer wouldn’t even think of.

  


#  **2\. VIDEO.**  


Become an expert at sharing your art with video. Use your phone—It’s good enough. While using the bare minimum, you may invent things that the typical videographer would never think of. Make videos of your art that make us drool for more of it.   


But the #1 skill you must sharpen if you want your art to get attention is…

  


#  **3\. COPYWRITING**  


The million-dollar question that I always end up asking artists who aren’t presenting themselves in the most powerful way they can is this:  


**"HAVE YOU EVER STUDIED COPYWRITING?"**  


Copywriting = The skill of communicating to get someone to take action.  


But copywriting, my fellow artists… Is an invisible art form. The better you do it, the less anyone will notice it. They’ll be too busy ACTING on the words you wrote.  


Most artists who communicate online aren’t copywriting. They’re winging it. They’re writing stream-of-conscious. They’re sharing “morning pages” vomit, hoping somehow people care. Don’t get me wrong. “Morning pages” are amazing. It’s a great tool to clear out your brain. But “stream-of-consciousness” babbling online is NOT the way to write to communicate with your community! Or especially with a jaded audience who has their guard up.  


Writing a video script, email, or DM… should never be done “off the cuff”. That won’t make it more “authentic”. It’ll just make it unwatchable, unreadable, or boring.

I don't want you to become a "writer" any more than I want you to become a photographer or a videographer. I want you to be a good communicator that get eyes on your work. Not making rookie mistakes, just because you don't know simple copywriting.  


You have to “catch them” before you can “move them”.  


### Copywriting is the art of writing to get people on your side.  


Copywriting is the process of writing persuasive words that motivate people to take action.  


Where could you benefit from learning copywriting techniques?  


**\- When you have to write an IG caption under your art.**

**\- Emailing a potential collaborator.**

**\- DM’ing someone asking for help.**

**\- Writing a newsletter to your fans.**

**\- Text for your website.**

**\- Your script for your YouTube or Tiktok videos.**

**\- Writing a bio.**

**\- Anytime you are trying to sell your art.**  


So, stop settling for the first words that come to mind, and then wonder why no one is responding. Instead, treat that initial idea as the FIRST DRAFT. Then…  


Apply the basics of copywriting:  


  * Learn the order of how to say things so they resonate.
  * Learn how to make your writing more quotable.  

  * Learn how to always have something new to say about your art.  




_Copywriting teaches you all of that._  


The problem is, most people who teach copywriting have a “sales-y” and gross approach” to it. After all, it’s salespeople and entrepreneurs who are their biggest customers. They teach you how to manipulate, instead of teaching you how to CONNECT.

When I was studying copywriting, I always had to read between the lines to find the more artist-friendly gold. But most creatives I know have no patience for that. That’s why I’ve been on this mission to find something more digestible…  


Well, I have two amazing resources for you.  


Packed with information that can help you. And presented in a FUN way.
