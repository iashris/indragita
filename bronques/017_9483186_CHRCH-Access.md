##    15\. Rai's Secret 

In photography there's a concept called the "what and the "how". The "what" is your subject and it is constantly changing. But the "how" is your style and that doesn't change. It's what makes you, YOU! It's your taste. But once in a while you get a "what" that is so brand new that you have no idea HOW to treat it. And that is when you learn and grow. That is when you expand your style.

This happened to me when I took my first photos of drug usage at parties. After doing it a few times and seeing the results, I realized that because my style was so reverent, I wouldn't be including these kind of photos in my work. That experience shaped my style. It showed me what I _didn't want to do_.

It happened to me when I shot my first day-time party. That first experience outside the world of nightlife, made me realize that it wasn't the time of day that defined my work but... the sexy and the rock and roll. That experience showed me what I wanted to _do more of_.

And it happened when I met Rai. She made me realize that I don't have to change my style just because she has an illness. When we did Part I of the shoot, I treated her with kid gloves. I wouldn't have done that with someone who wasn't sick. Rai deserved the same kind of boundless photoshoot. That's why I called her back the next morning and told he we weren't done yet. Lucky for me I got the second chance. And it gave her the opportunity to express herself in that grandiose way _despite_ her illness. All the same lastnightsparty rules applied: celebrate life. Tell her story. She made me realize that the world is ready for an alternative take on serious subjects.

What things are outside your regular "what" that can help shape and strengthen your "how". Seek them out. **That friction may help you get closer to discovering the message that you want to share in your art.**

Every time you step out of your zone of comfort, you won't know how to handle it at first... but push through so that you can discover the set of rules that make your style your own. 

Embrace the moments and the people that make you question the "factory settings" that you were born with.
