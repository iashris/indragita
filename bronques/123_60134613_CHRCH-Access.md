##    20\. WORLD BUILDING (PART VI): LANGUAGING 

When people say the word “snacks”, what do you think of?

Probably Cheetos, chips…  


But what if they say the word “appetizers”, what do you think of?  


Probably wings, crab cakes…  


But what if someone says “hors d'oeuvres”?   


Probably deviled eggs, burrata… The more expensive stuff.

Isn’t it funny how they’re basically all the same thing? They’re all “snacks”. But yet, each word gives you a different vibe.   


And isn’t it ironic how artists, the biggest “vibe creators” there are… Often use the most boring words to describe their art?  


Welcome to your new world…  


Languaging.  


Let’s start at the beginning.  


When you finish a work of art, what’s the next step?  


Sharing it, right?  


You share it with the world using the platforms you own (email list, website), and the platforms you borrow (social media).  


COPYWRITING is the strategic use of words to get people interested in what you’re doing. (You went through [THE COPYRIGHTING MODULE ](https://learn.makeartnotcontent.com/courses/enroll/lectures/58196471)already, right?)  


But LANGUAGING is using words to make your art more immersive.  


LANGUAGING is the words you use to communicate with your audience once they’ve already entered your world.  


It’s best described by example.  


When I was working with the 13lunas sisters (TMS artists), I suggested they stop using generic names for their magic potions… Instead, draw words from occult text. No more “perfumes and oils” like every other tincture queen with a Shopify account… Now it’s “elixirs, anointing oils and spiritual Banos”.  


[A vibe.](https://www.shop13lunas.com/)  


LANGUAGING is also how you choose to name yourself…  


I also suggested that the sisters never use the shortened version of their names anywhere online (Patty and Dani). And to only use their full names (Patricia and Daniela)… So they sound more… Regal.   


LANGUAGING can also be applied to how you name your artform…  


Are you a rapper like everyone else… Or are you an “urban poet”? Or a “verbal architect”? Or as Public Enemy famously said “the CNN of black culture”?

I introduced the concept of LANGUAGING to Theresa (TMS artist), last month. She’s building a marketing consultancy business for musicians. I explained to her that she should probably never use the word “marketing”, “consultancy” or “business” anywhere in her branding. Especially if she’s serious about being an alternative to all those gross generic music consultants you see online.  


She desperately needs LANGUAGING. She’s working on it. I’ll share/test the new language with you when she finds it. (Email me if you have ideas for her.)  


LANGUAGING creates a new world for you and your community. A world where you have your own lingo.  


But LANGUAGING is also choosing the words you will NEVER use…  


Did you see the video that Jack Conte (founder of Patreon) released on his social media a few weeks ago? He instructed his team to wipe all mentions of the word “content” from anywhere on their site, and all external communication. From now on, they will never use that word to describe what creators are sharing with their audience anymore. (We are winning, congregation!)  


[Jack Conte understands languaging.](https://www.instagram.com/p/Cx8TDaqLhrL/)  


And remember when Sean Parker (Justin Timberlake) says to Mark Zuckerberg in the ‘The Social Network’, “Drop the ’THE’. Just ‘Facebook’. It’s cleaner that way.”  


That’s also LANGUAGING.  


It’s time you start building a world around your art using LANGUAGING.  


Put the words you use under a microscope. Rethink each of them. Make them your own. Like this:  
  


**1\. Use words people hardly use.**  


**2\. Use old words in new ways.**  


**3\. Make up words.**  
  
  
Should you be calling your paintings “paintings”? Or calling them “snapshots of your dreams”.  


Is the place you make art your “studio”? Or your “lair”?  


Are you making videos? Or “cinematic diaries”?  


Are you just another “pop-punk” musician… Or are you making “revolution music”.  


Every time you change the words, you change the vibe the audience gets. You elevate your work before they’ve even fully experienced the actual art.  


**The words become A PART OF the experience.**  


Set yourself apart.  


Differentiate yourself.  


Look at my own use of languaging over the years. I even created a dictionary for my movement:

Without deeply considering it, I’ve been keen on languaging for y-eras. I call my at home art studio my “Scissor Temple.” As a nod to my mentor, Genesis P-O, I will sometimes tell inquiring strangers that I am a “cultural engineer” instead ov simply saying I’m an artist. In the Coyotel Church we would call “portraits” “POORtraits”, “paintings” became “PAINtings”, our monthly postcards created on the full moon were dubbed “FOOL MOON POSTCARDS” and our Coyotel “Saints” were S’Aints. Also you will never ever catch me spelling “ov” with an “f.” Not even when writing professional emails. My advice for people who have a hard time coining neologisms or arranging novel word sequences is utilizing the William Burroughs & Brion Gysin cut-up method. Ask the Wonder Killer about it if you are unfamiliar.
