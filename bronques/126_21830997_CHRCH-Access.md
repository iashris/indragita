##    22\. Are You Ready? Like For Real? 

## _Are you ready to connect with brands?_

Every now and then, people from various industries hit me up and ask me if I "know anyone that can X?"

Because of this, I've already been able to pass along some work to talented Mystery Students who were on my radar looking for jobs.

And now, more and more of the clients that approach me are willing to working with creatives remotely.

They WANT to give new voices a chance. (Thank you, pandemic.)

**So, I'd like to expand my current database to include YOU, if you're ready, so that whenever potential clients or collaborators hit me up-- I can recommend you on the fly.**

If you're a professional (or aspiring) creative:

video director

video editor

illustrator

author

photographer

singer

musician

model

trainer

auctioneer

music producer

barber

hair-stylist

copywriter

make-up artist

designer

producer

creative director

etc...

There are brands out there that need your help.

If you're unsure of yourself, use this as an exercise to see IF you’re ready to strut your stuff.

I can give you an honest opinion, without sugar coating anything.

_If your professional description isn’t precise, Imma tell you._

_If your work examples or presentation need an update, I’ll be brutally honest (sorry, not sorry)._

**Let's get your "portfolio" tight so I can pass it along immediately when there's a perfect fit. That way you're not scrambling... And end up losing the opportunity.  **

(But you know the rules... Anything I see, I may use as an example for other students to learn from!)

So if you're interested...

Send me an email - bronques@lastnightsparty.tv - with these 4 things: 

  


## **1\. Your city.**

## **2\. Your profession.**

## **3\. What makes you / your work special (IF you have a message this is where you’d put it)**

## **4\. A link to examples of your work.**

  


_If I can help you land your next big client or collaborator, that would make my day._

Here are a few guidelines for the answers:

  


## Your city?

The best artists create like René Redzepi (the creator of the #1 restaurant in the world, Noma)... With ingrédients that are in season and locally sourced.

The city where you live should inform your work. Use what's around you. You don't have to live in LA or Paris. Make your city your strength. What colors are around you? What unique people? How does your environment have a subliminal influence on your work? 

  


## Your profession?

Slashies beware... The more slashes you use IE: photographer / designer / writer... the more you weaken your "personal brand".

**Clients don't want to work with artists who are a Chinese menu.**

They want an artist with a voice. 

Pick your lane. Your niche. Your specialty. Your "thing".

I admit... You get more "no’s" that way.

But remember, you only need a few yeses to have a career.

And they will feel like you’re their soulmate.

  


## **What makes your work special?**

Interns, friends of friends, or the person you met on the plane... They need a short description they can tell their boss (or whoever is releasing the budget) to get them excited about YOU-- to make it impossible for them to choose someone else.

You’re a photographer, yes. But what is special about you? Maybe you “take the most debaucherous party pictures on the planet”?

You’re a hairdresser, yes. But what’s special about you? Maybe you're the trusted go-to stylist for the drag queen community in Austin, TX?

  


## **Examples of your work**

People just want to know that you’re professional enough to organize what you do into a nice presentation they can glance at and show other people. 

The way you present your work is one of the first ways you establish trust. You DON'T need volume. 

If you want to leave the biggest impression with your "portfolio" leave them with more than merely examples of your work... Leave them with an impression of WHAT YOU STAND FOR.

As you prepare your "portfolio", come back here often, and then hit me up when you’re ready to turn the world on it's head.

Why do I keep putting "portfolio" in quotes? It's to remind you to think out of the box. It may be a website. It may be your IG. It may be a singular video that tells your story.

OR... Maybe it's a NEW form of media presentation that gets people excited about your work even before they've seen the contents. 

You're a creative.

Be creative even here.
