##    The Artist's Guide To Storytelling 

## Storytelling has never really been about “telling us what happened”.

I watch artist's videos and I read their newsletters. And while on the surface, they seem fine, in reality, they aren’t connecting. Artists think they're telling us a story, but all we hear is "word vomit".

For blame, look no further than the creative writing courses we took in grade school. Those teachers did us dirty.

A better definition of storytelling is urgently needed today.

Case in point? Watch this video from The Black Wonder Twins (TMS artist):

  


Do you see what's "wrong" with it? Do you see what's missing to really make it connect with an audience?

Ok, now watch this one too from Mighty Eli (TMS artist also):

Both of these videos are raw and fun. They have good ideas. But bad execution.

They're missing the same thing YOU are when you make videos for your art.

**STAKES**

Learn that word. Memorize it.

You have no STAKES in your storytelling. But after today, I hope that will change.

The definition of storytelling you were taught in school is to “tell us what happed”. And innocently, you’ve been telling stories that way ever since. Not realizing that you’re boring your audience.

The truth is… That was never what storytelling was about.

# **Storytelling is about making us wonder what happens next.**

And you do that by having STAKES.

STAKES represent what a character has to lose or gain as a result of the story's unfolding.

STAKES are the driving force behind a character's actions and motivations, making the audience care about the outcome of the story.

STAKES can be personal, emotional, or external, but they are always clear, HIGH, and ESCALATING as the story progresses. (High! Escalating!)

Without STAKES the audience has nothing to wonder about, and you have nothing to tease them with.

Might Eli's video above has NO STAKES. And that’s why videos like these always fail to resonate beyond the artist’s immediate circle. You see, your immediate circle ALREADY KNOWS YOUR STAKES. That’s why when they see a video like this they love it. They applaud it. They tell you you’re the best. But to an audience that’s wasn’t there for your every waking moment, it’s just images and words. You have given them no reason to get invested. 

Same with The Black Wonder Twins video above... Never did I feel like something was hanging in the balance.

“This surgery could ruin my career.”

Nice opening line. But empty words in the end. Because they never told us HOW it might ruin their career. Actually, they did quite the opposite. They described how they have PLENTY OF TIME (5 months) before the surgery to get all their work done. Okayyyy??? And they even told us how they ALREADY have 130 songs recorded. So why should we care? Twins, you have 5 months to mix and master the songs and create the videos. Why is that hard? Where is the conflict? Where is the challenge?

_Where are the STAKES?_

STAKES is: “I have 5 months to write and record an album before I have an operation. Usually, I write a song a month. So I’m gonna have to find a new gear if I want to get this done. Every week I’m going to add a new layer of complexity so I can reach my goals.”

STAKES is: “I’m going to release this album, make videos, and play the online game to build my career after my operation. Even if I have to do it on painkillers and limited resources.”

STAKES is… explaining why the months before will be hard. STAKES is… explaining why the months after will be hard. With details that make us FEEL IT.

_When you make sure your storytelling has STAKES, you show you have empathy. You're saying that you value people's time and attention. You're signaling you want your time with the audience to be fun, not boring._

But The Black Wonder Twins and Mighty Eli aren't doing that yet in their videos.

And neither are you.

Ask yourself these questions after you write the first draft of your script:

_“Am I just telling people ‘what happened’? Or am I making them wonder ‘what happens next’? Do I have high and escalating stakes in my storytelling?”_

When you put your videos or newsletters through this microscope, you will see weaknesses immediately. Yes, it might even "bum you out" at first because you'll realize the whole thing is structured all wrong. But if you put in the effort to fix it, more of the right people will flock to your community.

Let me show you the difference STAKES makes. Here is the same video, with and without stakes. (Courtesy of Che, a TMS artist who went through the iteration process in WRKSHOP.)

Here is Che's video without STAKES:

I had to ask Che, “What are the stakes in this video?”

Her reply was: “The stakes are finishing this painting before I leave for my trip to Greece. It’s not about the deadline, but it’s about proving myself that I can hold the discipline and story all day way till the end. If I finish, I board the plane with closures if I don’t, I carry the weight of an unfinished piece.”

Ah, so she understands stakes (like I’m sure you do), but she still isn’t including it INSIDE THE VIDEO.

So I replied: “Stakes are not just saying, "it's important". Stakes is explaining WHY IT'S IMPORTANT TO YOU, so we can feel it like you do. Then we will root for you. You have to make sure you tell us THE CONSEQUENCES OF NOT ACHIEVING YOUR GOALS. Don’t assume we know. We don’t. Che, this video should have been about you explaining the reason finishing this painting is important. "I'm going to Greece and I hope I finish this painting on time" isn't stakes. (especially when the painting looks pretty much finished anyway) You need to make us feel it. Something like… "I have so many unfinished paintings. It's starting to make me feel bad as an artist, never finishing. And now I'm going on this trip to Greece. I'm not even excited about it. Not as excited as I should be anyway. And it's all because I know my self-esteem can't handle having another unfinished painting to add to the lot. It'll ruin my headspace. And my holidays. I'll be miserable the whole time thinking about it. That's why it's stressing me out so much right now. I have exactly 5 days to finish the (list what you have to finish), which would normally take me 2 weeks. Somehow, I have to fit it in every night after work. Let's see how far we can get tonight."

So she redid the video. And this is what she came up with:
