##    2\. How To Make It Rain (Part II) 

## **_“Business art is the step that comes after art. I started as a commercial artist, and I want to finish as a business artist. Being good in business is the most fascinating kind of art. During the hippie era people put down the idea of business. They’d say “money is bad” and “working is bad”. But making money is art, and working is art - and good business is the best art.” Andy Warhol_**

If you know anything about what I do, you know that nothing that I've ever done has ever been about playing it safe. Yet somehow I've also been able to work with the largest and most conservative corporations in the world. Usually those two things don't work together. It's either you're making artistic things or you're selling out by creating watered down content for brands. But I've never looked at it that way. I grew up thinking that art and commerce were supposed to be intertwined since all of my heroes intertwined the two. I'm talking about Warhol, Prince, Madonna. They all created art that pushed the envelope. But they also did it in this way that was commercially viable too. It was always clear to me that the two could co-exist. So that's just the way I think.

So when I started working with brands, I developed a way of presenting my ideas in a way to get away with the craziest stuff and get payed for it. And it was always win / win. They would always hire me again because I delivered. 

And today, with the internet, in an era where we have what's being called the 'creator economy', that balance of power is in the artist's favor more than ever.

But to use that power you need to be able to talk the right language. It doesn't come automatically. You have to think in a new way about your relationships with brands.

Once you start thinking this way, you're able to have very different conversations with people in suits. And then the idea of "selling out" is never even a consideration.

What's the definition of selling out? It's when you do something that your heart isn't into just so you can make money. And typically when you start working with brands, they slowly start asking you to do things that work against what you do to make it work. People who can keep their art pure while working with a brand are living "the dream". And most artists thinking that they'll only achieve this when they're super well known or have tremendous leverage some other way. 

Not at all.

You just need a new strategy.

  


## **_"The highest form of art is art that can help people achieve their dreams. Either by inspiring them or moving them into action." Father Bronques_**

When I've photographed festivals in the past, they would always hire a video guy and a photographer... and then... they'd also hire me.

I was never there as a technician.

I was always there as a rainmaker, 'cause I brought a whole new set of eyes to what they were doing that that the other people that they hired couldn't bring.

And I also another perspective that those people didn't have.

While they were shooting "footage"...

I was shooting stories.

The real FOMO is not in the footage, since all footage looks the same.

The real FOMO is in the stories that people are experiencing.

I was hired as a professional FOMO inducer. This made me a rainmaker for the brands that could afford to hire me.

I remember my first meeting with a Swedish vodka brand called Kanon back in the day. I did the weirdest campaigns and promotions with them. And they always went along with my ideas.

Why?

But I never had conversations about art with them. Harry and I approached everything I wanted to do from the angle of how those ideas would help increase their marketshare, or how using certain weirder concepts would help them get the attention of the people they wanted, and how these ideas I was proposing would get people to talk about them more. 

As you know, I'm all about art. And I'm all about making bat-crazy shit. But when I talk to brands I only talk about using art as a means to helping them reach their goals. 

Most artists? They focus on how "cool" something's gonna be , or how "pretty" it's gonna look.

But guess what happens when you leave the room? The creative department is only on little voice. They have to fight against the sales department and the marketing department vs. the lawyers who hate doing anything that hasn't been test... So you should Talk to the brand in a way that all the departments can see their vested interest in your creative ideas. Otherwise the moment you step out of the boardroom, your art concepts will go through iterations of watering down by time your trip in the company's elevator has hit the ground floor.

Change your thinking from making art solely for making yourself happy. To making art that can move the needle for a brand.

  


# Unless You Like The 'Old Way'...

Unless you LIKE knowing that the special low price you're giving someone "just this one time" will be the price bracket that they're gonna see you in..;. forever?

Or... Do you really want to keep working at a hourly rate. Giving them a chinese menu of services you offer... And basically just one step away from asking them if they want fries with that?  

Do you wanna keep saying yes to projects that start off great but get more and more watered down until they're unrecognizable. But then you're stuck-- and your name's attached to something that blows?

Often your clients will think they can get their cousin to do what you do for free... They never have a "budget" for your art even though they know you're "worth it". And that will continue as long as you keep focusing on the "content" and not the "context". 

The "context" is what the brand is trying to achieve.

You have to learn to create beautiful things based on those goals

They don't care about your "process" or your sensibility or your finesse.

They care about making money.

And once you start caring more abut making money, you'll be better equipped to help them make money too. 

You've just been talking to brands all wrong. Today let's fix that once and for all. Today let's talk about how you can keep the weirdness, the artyness, and the edgyness of your work in tact and make brands wanna pay for THAT instead of the watered down version they always end up with. 

  


# How To Best Communicate With Brands

The most impressive homework you can do if you're going to start working with a brand is to get to know the brand's customers more than the brand does.

Your art will be the main vehicle they use to communicate with that audience. And you should know who you're "talking" to.

Businesses don’t have the headspace to remember to walk in the shoes of their customers all the time.

But you? You’re an artist. That’s ALL you do.

Artists are the queens and kings of empathy. Our words resonate with people. So do our stories. Focus on THAT when you talk to clients... They don't need to know about your artistic "process" and all your touchy-feely rituals.

Don't make your conversations only about art.

Define what you're doing for the brand as "providing a solution to a problem they're having". 

That way every conversation you have with the brand keeps highlighting the problem they're having.

_And every conversation keeps highlighting you as the problem solver._

You just happen to be using art to solve this problem.

But don't focus on art with the money people.

Just because someone wears a t-shirt, doesn't mean they're not a "suit".

Talk to them as a business owner.

Tell them how what you will do will help their business.

Draw out a diagram where everyone can clearly see how YOUR art is directly responsible for creating growth or transformation for the brand. And keep that straight line in the diagram as your sole focus in all communication with the client.

Don't be a service provider, or a contractor. Become a partner... A co-conspirator. Their rainmaker.

**WHEN YOU'RE A RAINMAKER, YOU NO LONGER FOCUS ON YOUR TALENT, YOU FOCUS ON THE OUTCOMES THAT YOUR TALENT PROVIDE.**

#   


# **The Top 5 Ways Of Becoming A Rainmaker**

  


# **5\. GET KNOWN FOR A TECHNIQUE**

Become an artist that brings certain kind of ideas to life. And get known for that technique of yours.

So instead of doing the basics of your craft. Specialize. Switch your focus from doing what everyone else in your field can do... to focusing on a magical aspect of your craft that NO ONE can else can do.

Instead of being a photographer. for example, become a food photographer that specializes in set design of wedding receptions.

Your specialized knowledge makes you in-demand and may give the brand extra credibility, which means more sales.

  


# **4\. BE A LEADING VOICE IN A COMMUNITY / BE KNOWN FOR YOUR MESSAGE**

If you become a credible leading voice in a particular scene, when you get hired, you'll also be bringing the cache and credibility of a whole movement behind you that a brand, if they're smart, will be able to piggyback on.

You stop being a mere commodity to brands when you belong to specialized community that aligns to their values. Like a DJ who is a militant feminist, will be the first choice for the gig with a brand who's aligned with the feminist movement. 

Your associations make you priceless.

When you've carefully curated your choices, and made it so that all your body work has one clear message. You've positioned yourself so that any brand that hires you is tapping into that message by association. Your whole story and your message gives an added cache to raise the value of the brand. You become a defacto embassador for the brand because they've chosen you for your beliefs, not just your talent.

Try to work with brands who have the same message as you. That way your story becomes part of their story.

Develop a body of work with such a strong message that anyone you work with will immediately get anointed with some of that credibility. Brands will gladly pay for this added value that goes beyond just skills. When you work with brands who share your message, it lends BOTH OF YOU added credibility and value in the eyes of the community you both serve. 

And there's no predetermined price for that.

They'll have to negotiate with you.

  


# **3\. KNOW THE PLATFORMS**

All brands depend on media platforms for their marketing and promotion. So if you become an expert at creating for a particular platform, you'll become way more valuable to them than if they handed off creative duties to someone that works in their office. If you know how to make art that ignites the algorithms in flames, and is known to taps into the psychology of a certain platform, you will automatically earn your higher pay grade -- And now the weekend intern is no longer your competition.

_But the #1 way to become a rainmaker in todays creative economy. Even if you have no followers, no message and no community...  _

  


# **2\. CREATE ART THAT BRINGS THE BRAND NEW CUSTOMERS**

Your real client is not the brand. Your real client is the brand's customers. Get to know them better than you know your friends. Find out what makes them tick. Cause they're the ones who keep everyone's lights on. 

Learn how to create videos that show the product "in action. Learn how to photograph a lookbook for a brand that drives traffic to their website.

Use your talent to create multimedia work that will make people more curious about what the brand is up to.

If it doesn't do that, it doesn't matter how pretty it is or how cool the concept is... You've failed.

  


# **1\. LEARN HOW TO MAKE ART THAT HELPS PEOPLE TELL THEIR OWN STORY.  **

The most lucrative way to help a brand make money is to help them tell their stories.

Become a professional FOMO inducer.

Nothing attracts people more to a brand than a story they relate to. And the artists that know how to construct stories for themselves and for others are always the most in demand.

That artist can basically name their price.

Learn this skill and you will help brands connect with their audience. And the better you do your job the more devoted the audience will be. If you're a great storyteller, your art will help the brand gain fans for life.

In marketing speak that equates to lengthening the "lifetime value of their customer" (the amount of money they can hope to make from one customer) to much longer timeframes.

And that is the best rainmaking there is.

Rainmaking with yearly dividends.
