##    13\. Day 3 (Part II): The 'Personal Culture' Questions 

**Please take a break before this next section (PHONE CALL IS DONE, BACK WITH THE STUDENT).**

This break is best taken with the students favorite snack. Not a meal, just a snack. Oreo cookies. Ice cream. Chips. Pita and Hummus. Fruit. Whatever. Something small and enjoyable to them that will divert the mind a little bit.

You and the student need to bask in the glow of all this positive cultural analysis. During your break, keep talking about all the culture you love. If the student ever deviates the conversation to aspects of culture they dislike, nudge them back to the things they love.

  


**After the break.**

Now you will really challenge the student about their place in the world. We want them to become aware of the gap between their heroes and values and where they are now. Not in a bad way, but in an inspiring way. We want them to remember what actually motivates them.

They will want to stay on the surface. 

You may feel like a a-hole... But you have to continue to go deep.

You have to find answers that go to the core.

The first two questions are warm-up, low stakes questions. 

Ok. I lied. They're actually not.

These questions sometimes reveals deep dark secrets about what the student is most concerned about. Probe, but without making a big deal about it. But believe me their "inner critic" is going to go into high alert here. They may even be too embarrassed to share all of the results with you. Play it down. As long as THEY are face to face with the answers is all that matters.)

> **1\. What are your last 10 Google / YouTube searches? Why did you search that? (ask for each - probe deeply)**

> **2\. What email lists are you on? What makes you open someone's email. What emails do you save?**

  


Now move away from the computer. Bring out the list that you created during the 5 culture questions yesterday and place it somewhere nearby, in view.

> **3\. Why did you start doing this (art / business)? (You need to ask 1000 follow up "why's" here. Allow long pauses for reflection. If you don't link this to some childhood desire or something totally unrelated in their past then you're not going deep enough. Everything we do today is built from our foundations. You need to understand these foundations. THEY need to understand these foundation**

> **4\. Why do you get out of bed in the morning? (Don't settle for the easy answer. Allow long pauses for reflection.)**

> **5\. What is the single most important thing you could do right now that would have the biggest impact on your career / business this year? (Go hard) Why haven't you done it? (Go harder. Much harder. Find out the BIG WHY!)**

All these questions will make the student tired. The sugar crash from the snack won't help either. It might even make them frustrated. Allow this to happen, but don't get wrapped up in it like they are. Remind them that you're hear to find the truth. Even if it's hard. 

This is also the place where they will be tempted to quit.

_That's why you will need to finish off with something fun.  The next section is really fun and easy..._
