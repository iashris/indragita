##    3\. How To Get Out Of Your Comfort Zone (For Introverts) 

If you want to make great art… You have to learn how to get out of your comfort zone.  
  
If you want to create something that will move people, inspire them, and make them feel like it's the best time in history to be alive... You have to go to places where other artists have yet to go.   
  
**To be part of that 1% of successful artists, you have to start doing things that 99% of regular artists are afraid to do.  **  


"But Father, I'm an introvert…"  


That's ok. Be an introvert. Be shy.  


But be an ambitious introvert. And be shy EXCEPT when it comes to your art.  


Besides… I love that you're an introvert. Why? Because getting out of your comfort zone is 1000% more beneficial to the world when it's an introvert doing it.  


It's more exciting to watch someone shy do something bold than to watch someone extroverted do the same thing. The stakes are higher. The agony is more relatable.  


In this art-expanding module, I will teach you how to practice turning your shadow self "on" whenever you need it. (More about that later...)  


None of this is about being courageous… It's about being curious.  


It's about asking, "What if?" Just like you should be doing when you're making art.  


It's the alchemy of embracing "chance" and learning how to turn it into "serendipity."  


Here are a few shy artists who embrace "chance" as a color in their artistic process:

  


**Bo Burnham**

**Marina Abramović**

**Derek DelGaudio**

**Prince**

**Emma Chamberlain**

**Basquiat**

  


They're all ambitious introverts.  


With 3 things in common.  


They:

  


## **1\. Leave the nest,**

## **2\. have blind faith in the process, and**

## **3\. surrender to the possibility of nothing coming out of it.**

  


The irony is that once you finally commit to this "radical" philosophy, you will realize this is actually the least risky artistic path you could take.

For some reason that I have yet to understand— When you choose "the unknown," something extraordinary always happens.   


**Any time I've ever let go of expectations, embraced life's randomness, and used that energy in my art… It gave me a new career.**  


When you go that extra mile, far beyond where everyone else is going, eventually… People throw money at you. That's my experience. And that's what I've observed for the successful artists that I've been around.

Let me show you how.

  


#  **1\. "TRUTH OR DARE?"**  


Father says…  


"Always choose to dare."  


Choosing "dare" reveals the truth of any situation anyways. And it also reveals the truth of who you are.  


Choosing "dare" generates more luck around you.  


Choosing "dare" sharpens your intuition.  


Choosing "dare" is training for the future when it REALLY counts. It will prepare you for the day when a stranger that can help your career comes along (a gatekeeper or a potential collaborator). Approaching them will be easy. You will have practice and confidence under your belt.   
  
Choosing "dare" is the most important currency in your arsenal. It's how you achieve meaningful personal transformation.  


Choosing "dare" makes you understand the subtle but essential distinction between the "fear of something dangerous" and the "fear of getting out of your comfort zone." The first is the kind of fear you need for survival. The second is the kind of fear that will kill your artistic soul. 

If you don't keep putting yourself out there, what kind of art are you hoping to create? What kind of fans are you hoping to attract?

  


#  **2\. ADOPT A 'DRIVE-BY' MENTALITY**  


It's renegade season.  


It's time to adopt a "Drive-By" Mentality.  


Artists love to say how AI art is missing a crucial element: humanity. Yet, those same artists are afraid to talk to their neighbors. How much humanity are YOU bringing to your art if you stay hidden in a corner? Getting out of your comfort zone and interacting with the world is the biggest gift an artist can give themselves in the coming age of AI. It's the only fighting chance you have of surviving the Post-AI renaissance.   


Drive-By Mentality is when an opportunity comes along for you to come out of your shell— and contrary to your everyday behavior… You embrace the spirit of the moment.   


Your whole life, you were taught never to talk to strangers. And you listened. You were so obedient that it even became your personality. Always averting your eyes. Not realizing how much this fear of confrontation, fear of rejection, and fear of adventure has been stifling your creative potential.  


I was lucky. I had a teacher in high school who loved The Beatles. And he would always explain Beatles lyrics. He would use their words as allegories for whatever we were discussing. One day he explained the meaning behind the song "Eleanor Rigby." He explained how it was about missed opportunities. Two lonely people co-existing in the same small town. They could have cured their loneliness at any moment but never did. Until the sad day when their fate was sealed. Death was the only time they made contact. — That's why now, whenever my 'Eleanor Rigby' tendencies creep in on me... Trying to keep me lame and comfortable… I gag those impulses with a spoon and go outside to show the universe I am using my blessings to the fullest.  


"Talk to a stranger today."  


##### Dr. Robert Waldinger | The Diary Of A CEO Podcast — E246

  


"Talk to a stranger today" is the most transformative dare I can give you.  


Go and make a connection with a human being you don't know.  


Go lift the veil of fear that exists within your community   


This singular dare has an immense potential to change lives. Yours. A stranger's. And everyone else's that you inspire later when you share the story.   


Talking to a stranger makes you realize how small you were playing the game of life before. You'll wonder why you ever waited so long to start doing this.  


At first, it's scary. For the same reason it's exciting… Because you never know what a person will say.  


But whatever happens… The moment is always life-expanding.  


And you always come home with a story.   


It can happen on the street. It can be in a cafe. In a library. At a poetry reading. On the train.   


"But Father… In my culture, people don't like to be approached."  


"I live in a conservative town."  


Listen… I've traveled to the most conservative places in the world. China. Turkey. Beirut. Russia. And yes, people are different in different cultures. But you know what? They're also all the same.   


**I've never been to any city or town anywhere in the world where the people I met weren't moved by art, filled with passion, and didn't have a thirst to connect with other human beings.  **  


"Father, I do dare myself once in a while. I really do."  


Sure you do. I see you get out of your comfort zone… When you're on vacation… When you're drunk… And years ago when you had that customer service job...  


I'm not talking about that.  


I'm talking about "breaking the ice" in environments where everyone else (especially you) usually averts their eyes.  


Like, in line at the bank, or in the grocery store, on the plane, in an elevator, at the mall… Places where everyone is "playing it safe."  


You will get the true unpredictability factor by doing dares involving other people. 'Cause when human beings are involved, you never have any clue how someone will react.   


And with this kind of dare… The possibility of rejection is there.  


Ooh… The fear of rejection.  


**Fear of what other people will think.**  


Every artist's Achilles heel.   


It manifests in so many different ways.  


Fear of sharing your art…  


Fear of being criticized…  


Fear of people "not liking it"…  


Talking to strangers is how you practice being immune to all of that.  


I swear if you follow this simple prescription… Soon, you won't have to think twice anymore. You'll be so used to coming out of your shell on demand. You'll be able to switch "modes" on a dime.  


Yes, even if you're an introvert.  


Turn all those micro-moments that pass through your fingers every day... The "down-times" of life… Into artistic potential.  


All those times when you would usually avert your eyes—  


Avert your apathy instead.  


Ask yourself, "What if…?" Then go find out the answer.  


Watch what happens when you get back to your art stuff later…

You will have so much more of life to draw from. 

  


#  **3\. SHARE YOUR DARES**  


Make this your "new me" pledge:  


"I will never go a week without daring myself to leave my comfort zone."  


And because you're an artist… Document the glorious journey.   


Allow it to be messy.   


Allow yourself to fail.   


Then share the results with your audience.  


That's part of the fun. Watching even your losses become incredible stories. And eventually watching those failures become wins.   


I got my first photography job after only 3 weeks of taking photos. Even though I pretty much sucked. How? I photographed a random woman on the street and promised to email her the photos later (this is also how I built my first email list too, btw—PRO TIP). She loved the results. Lucky for me, she also happened to be a magazine editor. "If you can make ME look this good, I can't wait to see what you do with models…" She hired me. I did well. I made my first 400$ for photography services. And all because I approached a stranger! I have a million stories like this one. Right up until 10 years later… At the Oscars… I mustered the guts to walk up to Meryl Streep and convince her to go to the wine room so I could get a legendary picture of her with Taylor Swift.

Non-stop.  


I was still an introvert when I did all of this.  


An ambitious introvert.  


**When you talk to strangers, you start a chain reaction leading to amazing things in your career.**  


That's why I dare students in The Mystery School to come out of their shells.  


The ones who rise to the occasion always come back changed.  


"I didn't know I had that in me… Thank you, Father."   


Like Tiffany, who just released her new book… I told her… Please don't rely on only your book signings to promote 'Blue Hour.' Use life itself. Whenever you're in a bookstore, approach someone your intuition says could be changed by your words (just like you did instinctively after that first signing). Connect with them deeply (as an empath like you is apt to do), and then share the story on your socials so that your community and coaching clients can see your bravery in action.   


Like Alex, who paints and has lost her faith in humanity… DM and meet one of your IG followers for tea. Let that conversation percolate in your subconscious as you finish your next paintings. Strangers have things to teach us if we let them. They have the answers to our problems if we connect with who the energy points us to. It doesn't have to make sense right away. Let it make sense later when you're standing in front of a canvas 5 months from now, and you realize… "Holy Shite… Candice was right!" Then you'll also remember how it started. She was a stranger. Now she's your art show wing-girl.   


Like Dominic, who writes bangers and visual treatments for those bangers… Play one of your potential hit songs for a stranger on the subway. Notice their body language as you do it. Notice your own. This one or two-minute exercise will reveal more about how ready your art is for the world than any amount of sitting at home overthinking can ever do. (And yes, of course, film the whole interaction and share the link in your newsletter.)

Like Neena, who's a stylist on the rise… Go to malls and find people who look like they're on a shopping spree and offer to help style them and create a killer outfit. Document everything, and share the results with your followers. Show people what you can do off the cuff with little resources. Don't be surprised if someone hires you from this eventually. Don't forget… Your potential future clients are in your audience too. And they're watching your every move!  


Like Karimah, who's been trying to tap into the spiritual current to find the truest and wisest words that her soul can speak and combine it with her paintings… I'm like… Do your improvised spoken word for a stranger every once in a while… When there's that electricity of another human being right in front of you. And film your journey of getting better and better until you're freestyling poetry like @harrymacofficial on Tiktok!  


_Like Rowan… If I were a comedian like you, I'd approach strangers smoking outside of comedy clubs to see if I could make them laugh at my new jokes. I'd film my weekly exploits with all the anticipation and potential cringe of seeing how those real-world interactions turn out. And I'd continue the exchange and study the comments section._  


Like Kate, who is making videos for people to feel less alone… Take your videos further by filming more of those "conversations with strangers in a Dunkin' Donuts." Right now, you're trying to control the output too much. By always filming yourself alone, you're only reinforcing the idea of "aloneness." Defeating your message. Instead, inspire others to break their inner shackles by showing them what it looks like to throw lifelines out there… To not get bites… To feel despondent… But through sheer perseverance… To finally make a life-affirming connection.   


_Like Sofia, who has a new baby and less time for art… Go to a museum or art gallery once a week with the sole intention of making a new friend among the crowd. Or at the grocery store if time is restricted. Discuss your philosophies about art and life with this kindred spirit. Share those conversations amongst everything else you post on Instagram… Thereby returning your social media to the 4-dimensional representation of your passion that you want it to be.  _  


Like CJ, who's vowed to make his unique illustration style known this year— or die trying… I encourage you to go beyond drawing celebrities. Take photos of strangers to use as source material for your drawings. Then share the picture, the drawing, and a captivating story to document the whole process and inspire your ride or dies.

![](https://uploads.teachablecdn.com/attachments/IgF9UbNTa6GnT912Clzr_CJMONTAGE.png)

Like Amanda, who wants to share the art of body movement with the world… You're so talented. From this moment forward… Static posts are no longer allowed. Only dynamic media from now on. What would happen if you asked a stranger to dance with you in the middle of the street? And then you filmed the whole encounter? Do you know how many people are waiting for that invitation? Do you know how many people would be inspired watching it?  


_Like Daniela and Patricia, two sisters who make herbal magick potions for witches like themselves… I'd borrow some big plastic lab glasses and black lab coats and go out on the street and ask people what your collection's scents smell like. Film everything. Share the edited media online with your spiritual community to give them a "taste" of what you do and your personality. Courageously distance yourself from all the static Shopify stores selling "vanilla" herbal potions to the masses._  


I could go on. But you get it.  


Steal any of these dares for your liberation.  


Don't worry that you might be doing the same dare as someone else. If 10 creative people do the same one, it will come out in 10 different, compelling ways. Every new generation will put its spin on it.  


But these are only a few examples.

It's what I'd do if I were in your shoes.  


I would seek to operate at the highest possible level by challenging myself daily… 

I would make myself undeniable.  


I would make my online and offline presence a gift to the world.  


I'd use photography, video, and text to create the most inspirational media documenting my crazy journey of comfort-zone-busting exploits so that my audience could ride along vicariously through me.  


This is what I invite you to do.  


Create media showing yourself reaching for greatness. 

When you repeatedly come out of your comfort zone and share it with your community, it will inspire them.  


No doubt you've already been doing it, here and there, randomly. But those moments have been too few and far between to have any impact.  


 "Do it deliberately. Do it consistently."   


Repetition makes your fans realize that your courage isn't a one time only thing… It's a way of life.  


No one cares about your new album or your new painting (yet). They care about YOU… The art you create is merely a reflection of the life you're living.  


So if you want them to hang your canvas on their wall or listen to your music all day... Make your art symbolize the person you've become: Someone who isn't afraid to go all the way.   


**MAKE SOCIAL MEDIA YOUR LIVING, BREATHING DEPICTION OF HOW YOU'RE LIVING YOUR WHOLE LIFE: LIKE MAGIC. THAT'S THE ONLY ACCEPTABLE USE OF THESE OTHERWISE HARMFUL DISTRACTION-INDUCING PLATFORMS. LEAD BY EXAMPLE. INSPIRE OTHERS TO DO THINGS IN THE REAL WORLD. BUT MOST OF ALL, TAKE HEART IN KNOWING THAT BECAUSE OF YOUR NERVE, YOU AND YOUR GANG OF FELLOW MISFITS WILL BE IMMUNE TO THE "CREATIVE ECONOMY MELTDOWN" THAT'S RIGHT AROUND THE CORNER WITH AI ART HOPING TO DOMINATE.**  


I know you signed up for The Mystery School to take your art to a new level but guess what?  


The best way to start doing that is to take your life to another level as well.  


Sure, I want you to feel safe. But that doesn't mean I want you to feel comfortable. There's nothing comfortable about making great art.   


Your art can only reflect the person you are and the life you lead.  


Once you start putting yourself out there and interacting with strangers more... When you go back to your studio... Somehow, everything will just feel… Different.  


It might be a lonely path, but it'll be LESS lonely.   


Self-imposed dares are the re-energizers.  


Self-imposed dares are the energy boosts that come without a crash.  


##  **But remember… Pictures (or video), or it didn't happen.  **  


Master this. Then you're ready for the next level... [The Shadow Self.](https://learn.makeartnotcontent.com/courses/enroll/lectures/24395511)

DARES - A STORY My wife, her twin sister, and I recently went to a beer-tasting event. Hundreds of people. Hundreds. We're surrounded by people holding beers, talking, eating popcorn, and having a great time. My wife then starts daring her twin to do stuff. Apparently, it's a thing with them that they've been doing for years. "See if that woman will give you a taste of her popcorn," my wife says to her sister. And the sister does it. She gets the popcorn. Then she dares my wife, "Get that group of ladies to dance with you - you've gotta get all three dancing or no deal." And she does. They all dance. There's not even any music. I watch this, not only at this one event, but several that we go to over the next few years. Then I realize something. Since I met my wife, I've been doing more and more things that I never, as an introvert by nature, would ever have done. I never used to ask for the bill at the restaurant and she made me do it a few times early on in our relationship and now I can, although she still does it for me because she's so nice to me. She wanted to go to Hawaii to get married, so we did. And then after we're there and had just taken this nice nature walk to a magnificent waterfall, she said "So, we're going to jump off this waterfall, ok?" "I mean, you don't have to, but I want to, ok?" So I just went right up there and jumped off because I trusted her judgment. Afterward, she said "I can't believe you did that. I was kind of going to back out, but then you just went, so I kind of had to." Amazing. It is like she dared me, with an out but still a dare, and then I did, so then she did. "Why'd I do that," I thought. Well, the truth is, that baby step of getting the bill at a restaurant gave me just enough confidence to jump off a waterfall. I didn't think it could do that. I still don't believe it. But it's true. And as I look back now, there's been hundreds of things that she's subtly dared me to do, and I never knew she was doing it, but they've all helped me to do more and more things. Ever since I met my wife, my art has been on a steady upward trend. It's been getting better and better. I've been more assertive with my art, with my day job, with everything in general. I have more confidence now than I ever had, and I have her to thank for that. So, find someone who dares you and take that first dare. And don't stop. It's sooooooo worth it.
