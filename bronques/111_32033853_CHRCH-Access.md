##    10\. Why You Need To Be "Audience Obsessed" From Now On 

I can make a better burger than McDonald's can.

I'm serious. Ask any of my friends.

Maybe you have the same rep among your crew too?

So how come we're not making McDonald's kind of money for OUR burgers?

For the same reason some talented artists will continue to go unrecognized:

Because there is no effort being made to continuously expand our audience.

"Build it, and they will come" is a lie they tell you when you're starting out... To get you motivated. 

But if you spend the next three months working on your masterpiece and you never take the steps necessary to nurture your audience... 

You will release your art to the sound of crickets.

Your family and your friends might 'repost' or 'retweet' your new project. And you'll see some of the people you've collected, almost by mistake, on social media over the years do the same. But all that 'action' will only satisfy your vanity for a minute until you realize that nothing has changed. You still don't have a career. 

Why? You've been focused on making art -- and ignoring the most essential part of your business. Growing your fan base. 

And I know why.

Because the answers aren't jumping out at you after 5 minutes of thinking about it... So you say it's not "your thing". You know you need to do it, but don't know HOW to promote yourself. Maybe you've even convinced yourself that self-promotion is sleazy. Or you think it's gross and that you're above it.

But why would you expect this piece of the puzzle to come naturally to you? Self-promotion is just as creative as making art is. It requires practice too. And sometimes, you have to wait a few days before you get that genius idea. 

But you'll never get there if audience growth doesn't start to take more real-estate in your brain. And in your heart.

An artist can't sit in their "ivory tower" all day... Only to descend with their masterpiece every few years... And expect the "peasants" to line up for miles in anticipation.

Not even Drake does that.

A new artist needs to be growing their audience WHILE they're working on their masterpiece.

It's your JOB.

You need to be audience obsessed.

  


# **BECOMING AUDIENCE OBSESSED**

The most respectful thing you can do for your art is to create a foundation for it where your audience ANTICIPATES it's arrival.

And you do this by communicating with them with consistency. Fans are your family. You don't get to go away for 6 months and ignore your Instagram.

The 30,000 streams a day...

The viral video on TikTok... 

People reposting your last project...

They're all giving you a false sense of security.

None of those things means you have a career.

You only have a career when people are lining up to pay for your work.

Nurturing and growing an audience will give you that career.

You want them to be obsessed with you? 

Be obsessed with THEM.

  


# **THE ART OF BUILDING A RELATIONSHIP WITH YOUR FANS.**

If "being an artist" is where you want your money to come from... Then isn't it time you start focusing on the people who will be giving you that money?

You need to become "audience obsessed".

"Audience Obsessed" is when you switch from making "the best art possible"... To making "the best art possible for your fans". 

Your fans are your boss.

That's why you can't only think of them when you're about to drop something.

If anything... That's rude.

They have to be on your mind ALL the time.

Especially when you're in your dungeon creating.

You need to find ways to keep "your people" entertained. That way other people looking in on the fun will want to join in too.

You need to be obsessively growing that fan base. 

Not just 3 weeks before you're dropping something!

If you don't do it now, you'll only end up doing it later... When you go work as an assistant for an artist who IS taking their audience growth seriously. <kicking yourself when you see how systematic it all is>

That's why once you decide you want to make this your breadwinner... You can't only be "all about the art". 

You need to treat it like 'Business Art'. That's what Warhol used to call it. And Warhol is as 'about the art' as you can get. 

And what is Business Art 101?

Supply AND demand.

For all your life you've been 90% focused on creating "supply" (your art). But you've only been 10% focused on creating the demand (nurturing and growing your audience). 

Why would you give your art those terrible odds?

Posting about your new release on IG story isn't an artist creating a demand. That's just an artist milking their current audience the same way they see everyone else do it.

Even though in their heart, they know it doesn't feel right.

They haven't EARNED that attention.

Use your creativity to find new strategies to grow that audience.

The keyword in that last sentence is "creativity".

Some strategies may feel like a slog at first, but when they get you momentum, it's funny how fast they become 'fun'.

Some strategies aren't scalable, but they are the basis of what will be your rabid fan base. This foundation will allow for more scalable methods later. 

You need to treat marketing, promotion, and sales like they're creative aspects of your business also.

'Cause they are.

And these approaches should be in full bloom all the time. WHILE you're making your art. 

And if you play your cards right... All these things you're doing to attract new fans will excite the fans that you already have.

  


# **WHAT PSYCHOTIC BEHAVIOR AM I SUGGESTING THAT YOU IMMEDIATELY START ENGAGING IN?**

Most artists are on a cycle of:

1\. Make stuff for 8 months while they work a day job.

2\. Finally, release it and "see if it sticks".

3\. A few weeks later, they shrug their shoulders and start thinking about a new project. "This one will be even better!" 

That is not a career. That's wishful thinking.

How do artists get known today?

What works better than blogs, press, and even viral content?

Word of mouth.

A friend tells you to check something out and you will. We trust each other 'cause we know everyone else is lying.

And what creates that 'word of mouth'?

\- When you collaborate with other artists.

\- When you are aligning with other non-artists or brands who have the same message or a similar story as you.

\- Influencers that rave about your art creates word of mouth.

\- When you do extraordinary unscalable personal things for fans... Or when you stick your neck out to defend your community's opinion... Those create word of mouth.

Your career only grows when people are talking about you.

How do you create that momentum?

  


# **THE "MAKE ART... THEN" FORMULA**

Act like THIS every day:

\- Make art... Then go promote some merch.

\- Make art... Then figure out a cool collaboration you can do with an artist who has a similar audience (it doesn't have to be a bigger artist for this to be mutually beneficial).

\- Make art... Then DM your new fans. But wow them by doing it with a voice memo. Also, use some of these casual conversations as market research to find out what attracted them to your community.

\- Make art... Then reach out to gatekeepers via email, DM or through 6 degrees of separation. Reach out to any category of human that can get your music in front of fans. Bloggers, publicists, other artists, influencers, dancers... 

\- Make art... Then make and share a 15-second video or some heartfelt words that tell people a little more of your story and message. Don't overthink it.

\- Make art... Then share some behind the scenes of what you're working on with fans to rouse their curiosity.

\- Make art... Then test Instagram ads. Use things that have worked organically for ads. Paid advertising is best used for scaling. Once you have a message, story, and art resonating with a small audience, you now have the tools to use paid advertising to scale that resonance intelligently.

\- Make art... Then come up with a promo video for stuff you've done in the past (your archives are gold even if you're sick of what you have in there).

\- Make art... Then study an artist who's AT YOUR LEVEL that's doing well. Get ideas. Use what you learned THE SAME DAY-- as a test. If it works, add it to this "Make art... THEN" list.

\- Make art... then study the ORIGIN STORY of an artist like you who's blown up larger than life. Use what you learned the same day as a test.

\- Make art... Then give the audience a micro-dose of your art through social media: twitch, Tiktok, youtube streaming, youtube channel,

\- Make art... Then communicate with your fans via email. What will you say? You're doing so many things these days besides just "making art..." that there will be no shortage of things to say. Repeat your message and your story in a new way.

Once you put yourself in motion like this, karmic forces will start working in your favor.

You will start building a vibrant, interactive community. 

Before you know it, "people in power" will start to wonder what they're missing out on.

Keep fine-tuning this "Make art... THEN" balance until you make your "project" undeniable.

Make it so that when new people hear about you, they will HAVE to talk about you to their friends.

Your goal is not to get them to like this one piece of art. Or to be there for this "moment" you're having. That doesn't give you a career.

You want true fans.

Fans who don't only care about the art you've made.

But fans who's minds are already in line for the NEXT art you will make in the future.

True fans who are invested in you as an artist 

  


# **AND WHEN THEY LAND THERE WHAT WILL THEY FIND?**

Ok, you're slowly generating word of mouth...

This means that when they hear about you, they'll go check you out.

Either on Instagram (what most people do).

Or your website.

Or they'll Google you.

And when they click... What will they find?

YOU BETTER CONTROL THE NARRATIVE OF WHAT THEY SEE. THIS WILL BE YOUR FIRST IMPRESSION AFTERWORD OF MOUTH HAS PRE-FRAMED YOUR GENIUS. DO NOT LET THEM DOWN!

How do you kill them softly?

Marketing Speak: "Tell your story and spread your message at all your touchpoints."

All your touchpoints should be:

You at your most undeniable.

You at your most irreplaceable.

The moment people land on your media, they're going to make a quick judgment. Followed by a quick decision.

In under a second, they will decide yay or nay. And the only way you can keep them connected is with your story and/or message. 

But the best part is... You're not trying to get everyone to love you. So you can be outrageously YOU. The unique creature that will make "your people" will scream YESSSSSSSSSSS to the universe for finding you! 

That's why you have to make sure you communicate your story and message immediately. So they know they've found their soulmate artist.

Your story and message is what people will remember.

It's what they'll talk about when they talk about your art to get other people to check you out. The more your story/message resonates, the quicker new people will rush to experience your art for themselves.

Give them something to talk about.

  


# **CRAFTING YOUR STORY**

Crafting your story... is part of your art. 

Meditate on that fact until it's second nature. 

Your art is only ONE aspect of your whole package. Your art isn't only whatever you finished in the studio. YOU are also the work of art.

Beyoncé taught you that.

Basquiat taught you that.

Rihanna continues to teach you that.

You should have a master's degree in crafting your story after watching Drake do it for the past decade.

So tell me... When will you act on these lessons?

Craft your story. Or your brand. Or your persona. Whatever you want to call it.

Start now.

With everything you release out into the world.

Write the script for the movie that leads people to your work.

You don't need to overthink it.

Listen to how people already talk about you. There may be clues there. All you have to do is amplify it.

Sometimes the story/message is as simple as "She is the new Enya." Or "If Nick Cave was a girl..." And if people keep hearing that about you... that'll be enough for Enya or Nick Cave fans to get curious and find out for themselves if it's true. That's how some people got into Weyes Blood, for example.

You have to reinforce your story and message everywhere your art exists online. 

When those fans from the example above go to Weyes Blood's website or Instagram and see ethereal and dark imagery... They will continue exploring. But if they landed on bright colors and a cheesy smiling "LA influencer" looking girl, they'd exit out of there as fast as possible.

Notice how all of this may be happening, and they STILL may not have experienced the art yet. But that's ok. It's setting everything up so that WHEN the art delivers... That persona will be a fan for life.

"Creating demand" is about giving people who discovered you compelling reasons to keep exploring your work more deeply. And then share it with others.

It doesn't stop at words, pr, slogans, and bios either.

Your visuals have to back up your story and message.

Don't just put up random "nice-looking" promo shots of yourself. I mean, unless you're trying to portray yourself as boring and middle of the road.

Instead, create visuals that are like a movie poster for your story and for your message. Visuals that sell the particular story and message that you're sharing. (I don't mean your photos need to look like an actual movie poster-- Just make them tell a story.)

Your message and story is how you get new fans excited. It's how you get the attention of gatekeepers. Often these elements will be their first exposure to you before they get to experience your art.

Yes, through simple words.

If those words are enticing, it will pique their interest to make them want to experience your art. 

Giving people exposure to your story and message before they witness the art is called "pre-framing". Make sure the "pre-framing" is doing a good job.

Word of mouth is the ultimate "pre-frame".

And once they arrive at one of your touchpoints...

Get their fucking email.

That way, no matter what platforms you move to, you can always bring your whole community of fans with you.

  


# **HOW TO STAY ON MESSAGE (FOR THE CREATOR WHO ISN'T USED TO ALL THIS PROMO STUFF YET)**

Know the answers to these four questions like the back of your hand.

Ensure that all your "touchpoints" answer these questions right away so they get your "vibe".

1\. What's your name?

You think I'm joking? People fuck this up all the time. Usually, because they're shy. Who are you referred to as? Are you referred as a your GIVEN name, a PSEUDONYM, a BAND name, or a COLLECTIVE? Figure it out, and don't stray from it. It needs to be the same for everyone.

Everything else you share is what will give your chosen name "color". These are the things that will make your name memorable.

**2\. Why should we care about your art? What's your message? What do you stand for?**

Babe, there's so much stuff out there... Seriously, tell them only about the things that make YOU so unique? If you haven't got this figured out yet... Do 'The Drill Down Method' ASAP. Not knowing this stuff is hurting you.

IT'S TIME TO MAKE YOURSELF IMPOSSIBLE TO IGNORE

**3\. Where do you come from?**

As soon as people are interested in your art, they will want to know where you come from. That's just facts.

And like your "name", you need a firm answer that never fluctuates.

Either give them the truthful roots if that fits with your image...

Or give them mythology, if THAT'S your thing...

Again, be definitive about this. Don't give different people different stories.

Give them a powerful answer that reinforces your art, your story, and the message.

Everything that comes out of your mouth should reinforce your art, your story, and your message.

**4\. Who are my fans?**

You're creating a world. And every world needs inhabitants to stay alive. 

Your story and message is what bring new inhabitants to the world you have constructed with your art.

But who are those people? Define them.

How are you helping them cope with the world?

Where do members of this community, the ones who need you the most, hang out?

You need to know these things so you can find the rest of them and bring them into your world too.

And once they're in your world. Keep them fed.

Keep them entertained.
