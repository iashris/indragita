##    🔑 The Artist/Audience Relationship 

![](https://uploads.teachablecdn.com/attachments/3e4a313c18814459a023c0d76b8d56d3.jpeg)

**'The Audience Is Present', Marina Abramović**

When I ask artists what their relationship to their audience is, it’s funny how quickly they get tongue-tied. Yet still, somehow, they continue the delusion that their fanbase should be “growing”. Simply for existing.  


Pressed further, they might say something like, “People are following my journey to watch me make art.” Um, no. No one cares about your journey or your art. The relationship you’re describing is parasitic at best. And that’s why you struggle. You think it’s about you. You think you’re online to “promote yourself”.

Sure, while you’re in your room making art, it’s ok to follow Rick Rubin’s advice of “ignoring the audience”, if that helps you focus on creating.  


But once you decide to share your work with real human beings online or IRL, it ain’t about you anymore.  


Once you decide you want an audience to care about you after all, you’ll need to define what you want that artist/audience relationship to look like. You’ll need to DEFINE your role within the online conversation. And you’ll need to ASSUME that role so that people care about what you’re doing.  


The great thing is, it’s really easy, because there are only 3 types of “artist/audience relationships” to choose from. Three ways of communicating.   


Yes, only 3.   


1\. Inspirational  


2\. Aspirational  


3\. Transformational  


Before I go on to describe each of them, let’s get something clear. ‘Cause this is where most people get confused:  


What I am describing with these words is NOT your art (or your process). I am describing your “artist/audience relationship”.  


I am describing HOW YOU COMMUNICATE WITH YOUR AUDIENCE, not the RESULT of what they get out of it. You see... All 3 communication styles above can “transform”. All 3 communication styles above can “inspire”. All 3 communication styles above can make people want to emulate you.

But the other two results (besides your main communication style) will be BY-PRODUCTS of your efforts based on the strength of your art.  


The question to ask yourself is… Which “artist/audience relationships” / communication style should guide how you show up online AT THE BEGINNING OF YOUR JOURNEY?   


1\. Are you building an ASPIRATIONAL relationship with your fans?  


2\. Are you building an INSPIRATIONAL relationship with your fans?  


3\. Are you building a TRANSFORMATIONAL relationship with your fans?  


ALL successful artists, without exception, embody ONE of these relationships with their audience. Either instinctively or by design.   


The problem you’ve been having online is that you haven’t yet decided how you want to show up yet.

You don’t know whether you want your relationship with your audience to be inspirational, aspirational, or transformational. And so you end up sending people mixed signals. And confusing people. And so, they move on.  


There are only three types of relationships you can have with your audience.  


Let that sink in.  


Inspirational, Aspirational, Or Transformational.  


===  


An ASPIRATIONAL artist/audience relationship is focused on YOU.  


An INSPIRATIONAL artist/audience relationship is focused on THE ART.  


A TRANSFORMATIONAL artist/audience relationship is focused on THEM.  


===  


“Aspirational” means that you want to use your platform to lead by example. Like, you want to be so good at what you do that everyone wants to be like you. This is what influencers do. This is what celebrities and athletes do. And it's also what 90% of musicians do nowadays (they create "vibes" more than depth -- and that's NOT bad, it's just different).  


“Inspirational” means that you want to use your platform to ignite something that’s been dormant in people. That’s what artists who bleed something true in their art are doing. Painters, film-makers, and writers that rip you apart.  


“Transformational” means that you want to use your platform to help people change. Your journey is a surrogate for theirs. This is what mentors do. It’s what teachers and gurus do.

===  


Aspirational means… Who you are as a person moves us first.  


Inspirational means… The art you make moves us first  


Transformational… What you teach us moves us first.  


===  


Aspirational: “People either wanna BE you or they wanna FUCK you!”   


Inspirational. “Your art makes people cry (tears of joy OR sadness)!”  


Transformational: “You help people unlock new levels!”  


===

Are you getting it?  


So far, in how you present yourself online, you’ve been trying to be everything to everyone. And that’s why nothing is quite hitting the mark.  


Like, you’ll try to have an aspirational relationship with your audience and post like an influencer, but it’s not long before that fills you with utter shame.   


And so on other days, you’ll feel more like a pure artist, and want to be inspirational to your audience… And so you'll share your best art for people to see. But the truth is, your art only ever goes halfway, and more often than not, you play it safe. So no one ends up inspired by that.  


Or in other moments, you’ll be so inspired by other artist gurus and mentors online, that you'll try to be transformational to your audience, and share diluted IG-caption wisdom, while it’s clear to everyone who’s been watching you these past 3 months that you hardly follow any of your newfound advice.  


Stop this endless cycle of lameness.  


Pick ONE “audience relationship” to begin the NEXT PHASE of how you show up in the world (online and IRL). Pick and establish your main role for how you will start a healthy new journey with “your people”.  


Do this, and from now on, you will know how you need to show up for them online… And they will understand what to expect from you.  
  


# **Artist / Audience Relationship #1: ASPIRATIONAL**

![](https://uploads.teachablecdn.com/attachments/3be5d813a7814df6927df1c1ee021295.jpeg)

If you want to have an aspirational relationship with your audience, then you have to create a persona and/or a world that makes people salivate.  


Your audience will be attracted to you because they want to emulate some aspect of how you ARE and/or how you LIVE. You're not here to teach them how (that's "transformational")... YOU'RE here to make people dream by SHOWING them how!  


Like, maybe you have aspirational talent. That means the art you make is so good that other artists try to rip you off. And non-artists are simply in awe of your skills. (People wanna BE you.)  


Or maybe you have aspirational beauty (or an instantly recognizable personal style). You're hot. You're strong. (People wanna fuck you) Don't be shy about it. Embrace those gifts.  


Perhaps you have an enviable environment. Like, where you live is uh-mazing! Either your house or apartment is the stuff of dreams (or you make it look like it is), or the noisy city or rustic farm you live on makes people wish they lived there, too. (Living all alone in a cabin in the forest is just as enviable as living in NYC or Paris!)

Or you're wealthy.

Or you're poor, but you have a creative way of making your life look rich as hell.  


Aspirational could mean you are part of an art scene or community that others want to be a part of as well. Or that you are the poster-child for the times we currently live in (or a past/future era)  


Or that you have a sense of purpose that’s so clear that it’s enviable. People love your drive and discipline, as well as the fact that you’re on a mission. They wish they could be that dedicated to something.  


The more of these you have, the more aspirational you will be for your audience. The fewer of these you have, the more you have to be 10X exceptional in what’s left.  


### **EXAMPLES OF HOW THAT’S MANIFESTED IN YOUR ONLINE/IRL PRESENCE:**

(DOESN'T NEED TO BE EVERYTHING ON THIS LIST, JUST SOME)

\- You show off whatever aspirational aspects you possess on full tilt and as often as possible.

-If people talk about, or are jealous of your "art lifestyle" or your scene in the comments, it's working  


\- World-building isn’t optional for you, it’s mandatory.

\- Embrace of Regionality (you "rep" where you're from). 

\- You create a scene, or you join a scene.

\- You are the work of art as much as the art is.  


### **EXAMPLES:**

\- All influencers and athletes

\- MUSIC: most hip hop and pop, punk, and any other music that is more about the "vibes" than "creating something timeless that touches humanity"

\- Thirst traps  
  
\- Basquiat, Salvador Dalí, Frida Kahlo (if you don't know the artists' stories and about the time they lived, you're missing half the story with the impact of their art). Their lives are a work of art.  
  
\- Rick Owens

\- Movie Stars  


\- Pharrell, FKA twigs, A$AP Rocky, Dojo Cat, Sabrina Carpenter. Their music would have way less impact without their persona. The purpose of their art is to EXTEND their personal brand.  


\- Emma Chamberlain, Casey Neistat  


\- Kim Kardashian  


\- Tavi Gevinson  


\- early Bronques (as photographer, ticking ALL the boxes) — instantly recognizable personal style, lives in NYC, photographing the most exclusive parties, weird talent for disarming celebrities, part of the hipster art scene, sharing art every morning at 9am discipline  


NOTE: Artists who aren’t that original in their art, often rely on other aspirational aspects of themselves to compensate for it (beauty, wealth, connections). That’s not bad, btw, it’s smart. They’re playing to their strengths.  


  


# **Artist / Audience Relationship #2: INSPIRATIONAL**

![](https://uploads.teachablecdn.com/attachments/4c2953ab9fcc4436a5b8ca444dbaed7a.jpg)

Your personality isn’t the driving force in this artist/audience relationship. The art is.

You can even never show your face, if you prefer it that way.

But either way, the art has to be GREAT. “Good” isn't good enough. You can’t be making art that’s merely decoration.

I REPEAT: TO HAVE AN "INSPIRATIONAL" RELATIONSHIP WITH YOUR AUDIENCE, YOUR ART HAS TO BE OUTRAGEOUSLY GREAT. IT HAS TO BE UNDENIABLE.

People will gloss over this part. They will cherry pick from all the other qualities, and bypass this point. Like, yeah, yeah. But don't be the artist who ignores this truth to make yourself feel better. **Face what your role is for this type of artist/audience relationship to work:**

_Your art has to move people's spirit. It has to “change their life”._

Like others have done for you.

And how do you do that?

By not holding back anything in your art. You “go there”! Your audience expects you to take risks and uncover truths they've even had trouble expressing to themselves.

If you can't go to those heights with your art, then you should look at the other two relationships... "Aspirational" or "Transformational". With those, you can make up for the lack of groundbreakingness in your art by expanding on other worthwhile aspects of your world.

But if "inspirational" is the artist/audience relationship you want, you won’t rely on your personality or environment, or how much you can teach people—it’s the art, all by itself, that will grab people by the jugular.

You can choose to be as elusive as you want, as long as you’re emotionally raw and focused on communicating realness.

Your audience expects to experience extreme emotions when they experience your art: weeping, laughing out loud, rethinking their lives, and sometimes even giving them a desire to live life more fully.

You make your audience "feel understood". You make them "feel seen".

TO HAVE AN INSPIRATIONAL RELATIONSHIP WITH THE AUDIENCE IS THE RAREST OF ALL RELATIONSHIPS BECAUSE IT DEPENDS ON YOUR ART BEING IN THE TOP ESCHELONS OF YOUR FIELD. YOU HAVE TO BE WAY AHEAD OF THE CURVE, TAKING RISKS NO ONE ELSE IS TAKING, INVENTING A NEW ART STYLE, OR MAKING THEM FEEL LIKE THEY NEVER FELT BEFORE.

If you are a musician or a visual artist, you have to be a unicorn.

If your art is decorative... If your music sounds like everyone else... If other people can do the same thing as you... If you have no message... THEN YOU DO NOT HAVE AN "INSPIRATIONAL" RELATIONSHIP WITH YOUR AUDIENCE. You want to have one, I'm sure... But you don't. Sorry. You need to pick either transformational or aspirational to make what you're doing work on grand scale.  


### **EXAMPLES OF HOW "INSPIRATIONAL" IS MANIFESTED IN YOUR ONLINE/IRL PRESENCE:**

(DOESN'T NEED TO BE EVERYTHING ON THIS LIST, JUST SOME)

\- The first thing that strikes the audience when they come across you online is how original your art is. The second thing that strikes them is how your body of work feels like it has a message in it (whether or not they can articulate it).

**\- Online you share the things that emphasize what makes your art "1of 1". Maybe it's that you’re...**

**_1\. Bringing a new way of making art to the forefront (pushing your idiom to new heights). Or..._**

**_2\. You’re talking about things others aren’t talking about (politically, or raw emotions people are afraid to explore). Or..._**

**_3\. You’ve created a compelling fictional alternate world/reality for people to lose themselves in (as opposed to the "enhanced world based on your personal reality" that the 'aspirational artist/audience relationship' uses)._**

**You need one of these 3 things to be "inspirational". And whichever one you picked... THAT IS THE FOUNDATION OF YOUR MESSAGE! You needn’t look further. MESSAGE = you’re inviting a particular type of person (your people) to share your vision of the world.  Make everything you share online a mirror of that world.**  


\- The art you share has to trigger big, deep emotions. And you foster online conversations around those emotions and the art itself.

\- Everything you share online is about creating an emotional experience that resonates. You share art and media that reminds your audience about themselves. You are a mirror.   


\- You strive to connect all the people who feel deep emotions about your art into a space where they can congregate and communicate about THE WORK together.  


\- You share the process behind your art and how it connects to the emotions it makes your fans feel.  


\- You hint at (or overtly show) the behind-the-scenes effort you put in to bleed for your art.

\- You create a cause aligned to your art, or you join a cause aligned to your art.

  


### **EXAMPLES**

\- Aphex Twin, Aurora, Kendrick Lamar, Frank Ocean, ANOHNI, Leith Ross. Music is probably the hardest art form to have an inspirational relationship with your audience. 99% of musicians reading this aren't making music original or distinctive enough to fall under this category. Please don't fool yourself. Pick "aspirational" or "transformational" instead so you can prosper without delusion.

\- Early Weeknd (never showed his face, it was all about the music), then once he became popular, he added aspirational elements to his artist/audience relationship.  


\- Banksy  


\- Directors like Sofia Coppola, David Lynch, Tarantino, and Donald Glover. With directors, the audience doesn't care about "what you do in your personal life" (aspirational) or what your worldview is (transformational)-- unless these things directly pertain to the movie you’re sharing with us. Your contract with the audience is to make them feel deep emotions. That's it! If you can’t make someone laugh, cry out loud, yell at the screen, or shake them up for days, then you are not holding up your end of the bargain.   


\- Rupi Kaur  


\- Comedians (if you can make people laugh, you win). We don’t care what you do offstage (you're not aspirational). But when you’re sharing skits online, or doing standup, you need to make us laugh. That’s the contract you have with your audience.  


\- Writers. If you're going to be the voice inside someone's head, blow their brains out.  


\- Actors  


\- Petra Collins

\- Amy Bravo, Yayoi Kusama... Only Painters and illustrators who make art that is beyond “decoration” can have this artist/audience relationship. In other words, **visual artists with a strong message and A BOLD NEW ART STYLE.** 99% of visual artists reading this right now are not making visual art that is original or distinctive enough to fall under this category. Please don't fool yourself. Pick "aspirational" or "transformational" instead so you can prosper online without delusion.  


  


# **Artist / Audience Relationship #3: TRANSFORMATIONAL**

![](https://uploads.teachablecdn.com/attachments/5466d72959f94adbb8f13405358954c5.jpeg)

You are a thought leader, a philosopher, and your ideas and art are about inspiring people to change their ways by changing their thinking. But you don’t just imply it (like you would if you were inspirational), you are actually showing them the steps to getting there.

You see that “People Are Hurting” and you use art to help the world heal.

It’s not really about “teaching”. It’s about “activating” your community with new ideas derived from your artistic process.   


Your art doesn’t have to be groundbreaking, but your approach to creating it must be unique. You have a way of making art, a process, a way of thinking about it, that people want to adopt (even if they’re not trying to make art that looks like yours).  


You have an WORLDVIEW that inspires, and you show others how to adopt it.  


Your audience is in love with your mind. They don’t care what art form you choose. It’s the thought behind whatever you do that makes them see possibilities for themselves.  
  


### **HOW THAT’S MANIFESTED IN YOUR ONLINE/IRL PRESENCE:**

(DOESN'T NEED TO BE EVERYTHING ON THIS LIST, JUST SOME)

\- You teach people how to get to a new place in their lives

-If people are talking about how much you've changed their world view in the comments, it's working  


\- You embody what you teach in your own actions and art (almost “aspirational” but it’s your way of thinking that’s inspiring people here, not your persona)

\- Create a school of thought from scratch... Or align yourself to an established school of thought.  


\- Even if you haven’t arrived at your ultimate “teacher/mentor” status, you’ve figured out a compelling way to show your journey while you’re still learning yourself. You don’t have to have it all figured out to be transformational to others. You just need to be a few steps ahead of your audience for your online presence to be valuable to them.  
  


### **EXAMPLES**

\- Marina Abramović  


\- Rick Rubin  


\- KRS-ONE

\- Bob Ross  


\- Father Bronques  


\- bell hooks  


\- Virgil Abloh  


\- Questlove  
  


### **ONLINE**

Shantell Martin: [https://www.instagram.com/shantell\\_martin/](https://www.instagram.com/shantell/_martin)  


===  


“But, Father Bronques, I want to be inspirational.” 

Are you a generational talent? Unless you’re one of the art forms that is "inspirational" by default (writing, filmmaking), the chances you will have this artist / audience relationship is low.  


Sorry for the blow to your ego, but it’s not about “what you wanna be”. It’s about WHO YOU ACTUALLY ARE. It’s time to stop hating your true self, and trying to be something you’re not. You’re already special. Stop hiding that specialness. Start focusing on what’s naturally there and amplify it. Don’t try to be everything to everyone. Allow your natural gifts to come through.  


All 3 of these “artist/audience relationships” will inspire. Just in different ways. Like, look here:  


ASPIRATIONAL = you inspire people by who you are or how you live. They’re buying into YOUR VIBE as much as the art. That’s why the art you create will only be valuable to people if it is a vessel for your aura as a person. Eventually, people will see YOU as the work of art. If that sounds like you… Double down on it. Play your lifestyle up to the hilt when you show up online and IRL.  


INSPIRATIONAL = you inspire people purely with your art (who you are individually has no bearing whatsoever on your art’s impact). You can never show your face if you want to, and it wouldn’t matter because the art is so fucking good that people need it in their lives. Your art elicits extreme emotions. You are a truth teller, confrontational, and go where other so-called artists are afraid to. This is the rarest of artist/audience relationships. 'Generational Talent' type of rare. "27 Club" type of rare. And because it's so unicorn-like, it will require you to be ok with being on the fringes of society. If that really sounds like you, ok… Double down on it. Be raw and fearless with every artwork you show up with online and IRL.   


TRANSFORMATIONAL = you inspire people to change their being, all with your unique way of looking at the world and approaching art. People don’t want to copy you, like in an aspirational relationship… But they DO want to apply your way of thinking to their own lives. You provoke inner rewiring. If that sounds like you… Double down on it. Lead your people to a better way of being human and making art.  


BUT PLEASE UNDERSTAND... EVERYONE MAKES THIS MISTAKE... Aspirational, Inspirational, and Transformational are not words to describe your art.  


They are words to describe YOUR RELATIONSHIP TO THE AUDIENCE.

It's how you will FRAME everything you say to your fans online and off.

**IE.: "I want to tell you a story about something I did."**

**Aspirational = “I wanna tell you a story about something that I did that’s really cool and you’ll wanna do it too.**

**Inspirational = “I wanna tell you a story about what I did that made me connect to the essence of this piece I made.”**

**Transformational = “I wanna tell you a story about what I did that you can learn from if you’re ever in that situation.”**

**===**

Aspirational relationships make the audience feel just out of reach of the artist (you inspire your audience to dream).

Inspirational relationships make you feel connected to the artist without ever having met them (you make your audience feel less alone in the world).

Transformational relationships feel down-to-earth (you expand your audience's view of themselves). 

===

In aspirational relationships, the main character is you, your scene, and your city.

In inspirational relationships, the main character is your art and the emotions it brings.

In transformational relationships, the main character is your audience and the epiphanies they receive through your worldview. 

===

Doubling down on your artist/audience relationship means framing everything you share with them through the same lens, so they know what to expect, and you always have clarity on how to show up for them.  


If you’re brutally honest with yourself, you’ll see that ONE of these communication styles is more natural to you than the other two. CHOOSE IT. And then, double down on it! Show up for your audience with intention and with your proper communication style.  


As soon as you pick what that relationship will be, you’ll notice just how much you’ve been sending your audience mixed signals, leaving your followers confused and disengaged.   


Every artist succeeding online has doubled down on their artist/audience relationship. None of them is wishy-washy about it. They are not shy about playing their role to the maximum.  


_Know how you will show up, so your audience knows what to expect._

Yes, you have it right. All the lenses could apply to all artists. But if an artist wants their online presence to be as strong as possible, as they're still finding their audience, it's best to choose one lens to filter everything through. Bradley, I don't see you as "transformational" since naturally, I've never seen you take a mentor/teacher role with your art. Or ever heard you push a worldview. Your best look would be "inspirational" or "aspirational". The music artists best suited to the "inspirational" communication style are artists who have an audience that believes they've "invented a new form of music".These artists' music can usually speak for itself without any other story. Rare breed. That's why most music artists nowadays choose an "aspirational" relationship with their audience instead. They create a world around their music, they tell stories of the music, use fashion, create a regional scene around, and an online communit etc. All the things I described above. They use the whole "vibe" to create the emotional impact.

> It's not about “what you wanna be”. It’s about WHO YOU NATURALLY ARE. It’s time to stop hating your true self This was my challenge for the longest time. I was trying to not show my face and still have my art generate fans and get love from them. I was craving love deep down but my ego was like "if you show ur face or ur real self, that's lame, u can't make art good enough that stands on it's own"? I thought about this and I've concluded that this mindset of "if ur not belting C5 with ease, making songs like Pink Floyd straight out the gate, foh" comes from my upbringing in the middle class, in a small town in a "third world country". Even to this day, the "all or nothing" thinking is prevalent in people in my culture regarding art and mastery. The whole culture is myopic but that smells like an opportunity to me more than anything! And accepting the fact that maybe my art isn't on House Of Balloons level yet, and that I'd have to wait a few more years before it may get to that level, was tough. I realized that getting love from my fans and building a community/world around me and my art was more urgent than feeding my ego by being the next The Weeknd or Frank Ocean. So, Aspirational relationship it is! Me and my art are like bread and butter. We go together now 🦢♥️ This was an incredibly practical and transformative lesson, thank you Father Bronques! ✞✞
