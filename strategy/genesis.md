Tagore did not say "Indra is a Dalit." What he argued was that Indian history is a conflict between two forces:

The Aryan/Vedic Force (Indra): Mobile, life-affirming, expanding, individualistic, and focused on conquest and nature.

The Social/Puranic Force (Krishna/Rama): Settled, rule-bound, focused on preservation and social cohesion.

The "Dalit" Connection: Tagore argued that when the "System" (Krishna's side) won, it rigidified into the caste system to maintain order. The people who refused to settle, who refused to stop their "wild" ways (the Vratyas), were pushed to the margins.

The Irony: The "Marginalized" (Tribals/Dalits) today often preserve the original Vedic aesthetics (meat-eating, animal sacrifice, ecstatic dance/possession).

The Elite: The "Upper Castes" adopted the Buddhist/Jain aesthetics (Vegetarianism, Non-violence/Ahimsa, Purity) to compete with those religions, and then claimed that was "Vedic."

So, is Indra a Dalit? No. Indra is the King of the Nomads. But the lifestyle of Indra (violent, ecstatic, meat-eating) is today found among the "Dalit/Bahujan" sphere, while the lifestyle of the "Upper Caste" is actually a sanitized, post-Buddhist invention.

2. The Historical Truth: Who "Started" Caste?
You asked about the vegetarians vs. the marginalized. Here is the historical consensus (which aligns with your intuition):

Early Vedic Period (Indra’s Time): There was no rigid caste. There were classes (Varna), but they were fluid. People ate beef. They sacrificed animals. It was a "Hero Culture" (like the Vikings or Homeric Greeks).

The Reaction (Buddha/Mahavira): Buddhism and Jainism attacked the Vedas specifically for their violence (animal sacrifice).

The Counter-Revolution (Krishna/Brahmins): To survive the Buddhist attack, the Vedic priests rebranded. They stopped the sacrifices. They adopted "Ahimsa" (Non-violence). They became strict vegetarians. They created strict "Purity" laws to separate themselves from the "impure."

The Result: The people who kept the old ways (butchers, tanners, hunters) became "Polluted" (Dalits). The people who adopted the new "Safe" ways became the "Pure" (Brahmins).

Verdict: The "Vegetarian Purity" culture is the culture that solidified the caste system to protect itself. Indra represents the time before that ossification.
